# Anime-Action Hook Framework

The first 2 seconds of an anime clip must immediately establish the anime aesthetic and
capture attention through visual impact. This reference documents 12 anime hook techniques
and a selection guide.

## Why the 2-Second Hook Matters in Anime

Anime openings are engineered to demand continued viewing. The first 2 seconds must:

- Establish the anime genre (shonen action vs. magical girl vs. mecha vs. slice-of-life)
- Create visual impact through a signature anime technique
- Make the viewer understand immediately that this is animation, not a static image
- Convey the emotional tone of the piece

When animating from a user-supplied frame, the hook also signals that the source image
has come alive — the transition from still to motion must be unmistakable.

## Twelve Anime Hook Techniques

### 1. Dramatic Eye Close-Up with Light Reflection

Open on an extreme close-up of a character's eye. The iris shows detail — star-shaped
highlights, color gradations, or a tiny reflection of flames/action. Hold 0.5 s of
stillness, then pupil contracts or expands as the scene "activates."

**Best for:** Power reveals, emotional moments, establishing a character's presence
**Prompt:** "Extreme close-up of [character]'s eye. Iris [color] with [star/cross]-shaped
highlights. 0.5 s stillness, then pupil [contracts/expands] rapidly at [timestamp]."

### 2. Speed Line Burst

Straight lines (perspective lines) emanate from the center of frame or from an object,
indicating sudden motion or a moment about to happen. Combine with slight zoom-in.

**Best for:** Action openings, fast transitions, moments of sudden movement
**Prompt:** "Speed lines burst from [origin point], [radial/horizontal] pattern. Slight
zoom-in accompanies the burst. Lines clear at [timestamp]."

### 3. Transformation Sequence Flash

A flash of white light obscures the screen (0.3 s max), then pulls back to reveal a
transformed state — new outfit, powered-up form, or shifted character.

**Best for:** Power-up sequences, costume reveals, character transformations
**Prompt:** "White flash obscures screen instantaneously, disorienting for 0.3 s, then
pulls back revealing [transformed state]. Flash clears at 0.3 s."

### 4. Blade Unsheath with Metal Gleam

A sword, weapon, or object drawn with a metallic shine. At the moment the blade clears
the sheath, a brilliant light reflection flashes across it. Pairs with slight motion
slowdown.

**Best for:** Samurai, action, historical, fantasy anime
**Prompt:** "Blade drawn from sheath with precise motion. Metallic gleam flashes across
blade at clearance point. Slight slow-motion at gleam. Speed lines follow blade path."

### 5. Power-Up Aura Explosion

Colored energy (electric blue, golden, or purple) expands outward from a character in
layers with particles trailing. The expansion pushes outward with force, momentarily
distorting the background.

**Best for:** Battle anime, power reveals, charged moments
**Prompt:** "[Color] aura expands from [character] in [count] concentric rings. Particles
trail at aura surface. Air distorts around the expansion. Holds [X] s before settling."

### 6. Cherry Blossom Wind Gust

Petals swirl across frame on a gust of wind, establishing romance or nostalgia mood.
Petals are semi-translucent, floating on invisible currents, possibly obscuring/revealing
elements behind.

**Best for:** Romance, slice-of-life, emotional farewell, nostalgic moments
**Prompt:** "Semi-translucent pink/white cherry blossoms drift across frame from [direction].
Petals catch [cool/warm] light. Behind petals: [scene description] at 50% blur."

### 7. Anime Title Card Slam

Bold stylized text (katakana, kanji, or English) appears with dynamic perspective, rotating
into place from off-screen. Background may shift or blur. Text has bold outline and fill.

**Best for:** Opening sequences, episode titles, show intros
**Prompt:** "Bold anime-style title enters from [direction] with perspective rotation,
slamming into center frame at [timestamp]. Speed lines behind text. [Color] fill with
black outline."

### 8. Character Silhouette Against Dramatic Sky

Character or object in silhouette against golden sunset, blue twilight, or storm clouds.
Sky fills 60–70% of frame with dramatic color gradients. Silhouette is perfectly black.

**Best for:** Establishing scale, tone, and character gravity
**Prompt:** "Character silhouette perfectly black against [sky type]. Sky fills 65% of
frame in [color gradient]. Rim lighting [color] barely visible on silhouette edges."

### 9. Impact Frame with Cross-Shaped Highlight

A moment of physical impact frozen in time with a bright cross-shaped highlight at center.
Surrounding area may blur or motion-blur backward. Holds 0.2–0.4 s.

**Best for:** Action climaxes, fight openers, collision moments
**Prompt:** "Impact frozen at [timestamp]. Cross-shaped highlight bright [color] at impact
point. Surrounding motion-blurs backward. Frame holds [X] s before releasing."

### 10. Chibi Reaction Pop

Character appears in exaggerated chibi proportion (larger head, smaller body) with
oversized expression — surprise, anger, or cuteness. Accompanied by quick pulsing glow.

**Best for:** Comedy, light anime, comedic reaction moments
**Prompt:** "Character pops to chibi proportions at [timestamp]. Oversized head, tiny
body. Expression: [shock/anger/cuteness]. Quick glow pulse around character."

### 11. Sweat Drop Reaction

A single bead of sweat appears on a character's face/head, reflecting light, and falls.
Implies nervousness, effort, or comedy. Drop should be glossy and catch light realistically.

**Best for:** Comedy, nervous moments, rom-com beats
**Prompt:** "Glossy sweat drop forms at [location] over 0.5 s, catching light with
bright reflection. Drops slowly. Implies [nervousness/comedy/effort]."

### 12. Screen Tone Shift

The entire image momentarily overlays with a halftone pattern or diagonal lines,
emphasizing emotion or comedic moment. Lasts 0.1–0.3 s before clearing.

**Best for:** Manga-style emphasis, emotional beats, comedic beats
**Prompt:** "Halftone/diagonal line screen tone overlays frame at [timestamp] for [X] s.
Emphasizes [emotion/comedy]. Clears back to scene."

## Hook Selection Guide

| Anime genre / content | Recommended hooks |
|----------------------|------------------|
| Shonen action | Speed Line Burst (#2), Power-Up Aura (#5), Impact Frame (#9) |
| Seinen drama | Eye Close-Up (#1), Character Silhouette (#8), Screen Tone (#12) |
| Magical girl | Transformation Flash (#3), Cherry Blossom (#6), Chibi Reaction (#10) |
| Mecha | Impact Frame (#9), Speed Line Burst (#2), Power-Up Aura (#5) |
| Slice-of-life | Cherry Blossom (#6), Chibi Reaction (#10), Sweat Drop (#11) |
| Romance / emotional | Eye Close-Up (#1), Cherry Blossom (#6), Silhouette (#8) |
| Isekai / fantasy | Transformation Flash (#3), Power-Up Aura (#5), Title Card (#7) |
| Cyberpunk / sci-fi | Speed Line Burst (#2), Impact Frame (#9), Aura Explosion (#5) |
| Anime opening (OP) | Title Card Slam (#7), Speed Line Burst (#2), Transformation Flash (#3) |
| Anime ending (ED) | Cherry Blossom (#6), Character Silhouette (#8), Eye Close-Up (#1) |
