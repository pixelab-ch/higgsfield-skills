# Real Estate Prompt Examples

Master template and worked prompts for property showcase video generation via
`cinematic_studio_3_0` (primary) and `veo3_1` (fallback). All prompts follow the
2-second hook framework from `references/hooks.md` and the walkthrough craft from
`references/walkthrough-craft.md`.

## Master Template

Use this structure for any property video:

```
MODEL: cinematic_studio_3_0 (or veo3_1 if fallback applies)
ASPECT RATIO: [16:9 / 9:16 / 1:1]
DURATION: [4–15 s]

[2-SECOND HOOK]
Opening 0–2 s: [Hook type] — [What appears on screen and viewer emotional response]

[ESTABLISHING CONTEXT]
Location and neighborhood context; drone aerial or approach.
Property type, style, and first impression.

[ARRIVAL AND ENTRY]
Approach sequence; front door threshold moment.
Initial interior reveal — ceiling height, light quality, or view.

[PRIMARY SPACES]
Living spaces: camera movement type, what to reveal, pacing.
Kitchen and dining: function and view.
Bedroom and bathroom: intimacy, views, luxury finishes.

[SPECIALTY AND OUTDOOR SPACES]
Any unique features: pool, view, specialty rooms.
Outdoor entertaining areas; drone aerial of grounds.

[EVENING OR TWILIGHT SEQUENCE]
Day-to-night transition showing 24-hour appeal.
Interior lights glowing; exterior architecture illuminated.

[FINAL STATEMENT]
Return to most iconic view or feature.
Music swells; emotional resonance.
Overlay: address, agent contact, call-to-action.

MUSIC: [Style, tempo, reference artists]
STYLE TONE: [Aspirational luxury / contemporary urban / etc.]
DURATION: [Target runtime]
```

## Example 1: Luxury Waterfront Home

**Model:** `cinematic_studio_3_0`
**Aspect ratio:** `16:9`
**Duration:** `15` s (short-form version — adapt for longer runtime in full production)

**HOOK:** Drone begins high above property, revealing expansive grounds, mature landscaping,
and the shimmering ocean beyond. As the drone descends, the full architectural scope becomes
clear: modern Mediterranean villa on elevated bluff overlooking water. Descent is slow and
luxurious.

**ESTABLISHING CONTEXT:** Smooth gimbal glide along tree-lined driveway toward property.
Golden hour light (early evening, 5 PM) casts long shadows and bathes facade in warm honeyed
light. Approach front entry; heavy wooden doors inset with glass invite entry.

**ARRIVAL:** Doors open slowly. Cross threshold into soaring two-story foyer with marble floor
and a view straight through to the ocean beyond. Late afternoon light creates geometric patterns
on floor. Camera rises slightly, looking up to vaulted ceiling to communicate height and light.

**PRIMARY SPACES:** Glide through great room — fireplace as focal point, but the true focal
point is the view. Camera moves toward floor-to-ceiling windows. Slow approach to glass; ocean
horizon fills the frame. Stay here, panning across the view for 4–5 seconds. Let it breathe.

**Kitchen and dining:** Waterfront view above the sink. White marble counters, stainless
appliances, island as entertaining hub. Dining table positioned to look at the ocean during
meals. Detail shot of marble veining in raking light.

**Bedroom and bath:** Master bedroom with ocean-view bed. Transition to spa-like master bath
with soaking tub at window — bathing while overlooking the ocean. Marble surrounds and luxury
fixtures emphasized.

**OUTDOOR SPACES:** Infinity pool deck — pool edge blends with horizon line; water and sky
appear to merge. Lounge seating; mature specimen trees for privacy; 180-degree ocean view.
Drone rising above pool showing entertaining footprint and property's relationship to coastline.

**EVENING TRANSFORMATION:** Transition to twilight (30 minutes after sunset). Exterior accent
lighting creates drama. Interior lights glow warm against deep blue sky. Pool illuminated;
property transforms from daytime elegance to evening luxury.

**FINAL STATEMENT:** Return to the view — long lingering shot of ocean at twilight.
Music swells. Overlay: address, agent contact, call-to-action.

**Music:** Ambient, spacious, minimal — Ólafur Arnalds or Max Richter style. Slight ocean
ambience layered beneath. Build subtly during key reveals.
**Tone:** Ultra-luxury, aspirational. Every frame communicates exclusivity and refined taste.

## Example 2: Modern Downtown Luxury Apartment

**Model:** `cinematic_studio_3_0`
**Aspect ratio:** `16:9`
**Duration:** `15` s (short-form version)

**HOOK:** Point-of-view shot from inside apartment, looking directly out floor-to-ceiling
window. Camera pulls back slowly, revealing sleek interior — minimalist white and light gray
palette, polished concrete flooring, modern furnishings. Cityscape fills the window behind
the interior. Within 2 s, viewer understands: urban sanctuary with serious views.

**WINDOWS AND VIEWS:** Camera glides toward window wall; city beyond dominates the frame.
Pan across skyline slowly — buildings, traffic, urban energy visible. Shoot at twilight
(blue hour) when city lights illuminate; urban landscape becomes jewel-like at night.

**INTERIOR SPACES:** Open-plan living with floor-to-ceiling window view dominating.
Minimal furnishings — geometric sofa, single piece of art. Slow 180° orbit showing how
space relates to view and how light moves across polished concrete floor.

**Kitchen:** Galley or island kitchen with view visible beyond. Stainless steel appliances,
quartz or marble counters, minimal hardware. Detail shot: marble veining in sharp light.

**Bedroom:** Bed positioned to see the city; morning light floods the space. A private
sleep space within an urban high-rise — rest and cityscape together.

**Bathroom:** Modern spa-like with geometric tile, floating vanity, rainfall showerhead.
Minimal palette matches the apartment. Retreat space with luxury fixtures.

**EVENING REVEAL:** Interior lights on; exterior city dark and glittering with lights.
Apartment glows warmly. Contrast between warm interior and cool city beyond.

**FINAL STATEMENT:** Return to window. Camera approaches slowly and stops; viewer sees
out into glittering city. Music swells. Overlay: building name, amenities, agent contact.

**Music:** Sophisticated, contemporary — jazz-inflected electronic (Jon Hopkins, Tycho style).
Tempo matches urban rhythm — moderate, purposeful.
**Tone:** Sophisticated, contemporary, exclusive. Minimalist oasis elevated above the city.

## Example 3: Commercial Office Space

**Model:** `cinematic_studio_3_0`
**Aspect ratio:** `16:9`
**Duration:** `12` s (short-form version)

**HOOK:** Interior shot showing open-plan office space flooded with natural light. Sunlight
rakes across space, creating geometric shadows on collaborative desks. Within 2 s, viewer
understands: high-quality, light-filled, thoughtfully designed workspace.

**FOYER AND RECEPTION:** Minimalist modern design, branded elements subtle. Floor-to-ceiling
windows showing city context. Natural light is key. Camera glides through; professional
atmosphere apparent.

**OPEN PLAN WORKSPACE:** Camera moves through space showing modular desk arrangements,
abundant natural light, technology infrastructure (subtle), ergonomic seating, acoustic
paneling, biophilic design elements (greenery, natural materials).

**COLLABORATION AND CONFERENCE:** Open conference areas, informal gathering zones, glass-walled
conference rooms with modern A/V seamlessly integrated. Sight lines between zones visible.

**AMENITY SPACES:** Break room / café humanizes the space. Wellness areas (if present)
communicate forward-thinking tenant experience.

**EVENING TRANSFORMATION:** Office lights create warm glow. Space remains inviting and
professional at night; 24-hour appeal evident.

**FINAL STATEMENT:** Sweeping view of full open-plan space. Music reaches positive crescendo.
Overlay: available square footage, amenities, broker contact.

**Music:** Professional, forward-thinking contemporary instrumental — moderate pace, energy
and optimism without being frenetic.
**Tone:** Professional, aspirational, competent. Light and openness as key selling features.

## Example 4: Renovation Before / After Transformation

**Model:** `cinematic_studio_3_0`
**Aspect ratio:** `16:9`
**Duration:** `14` s (short-form version; before 4 s / after 10 s)

**HOOK:** BEFORE: dated finishes, worn surfaces, dark lighting, cramped layout (0–1 s).
Cut immediately to AFTER: same space newly renovated — light floods in, modern finishes
shine, space feels twice as large (1–2 s). Quick contrast plants the question: what was
changed and how dramatically?

**BEFORE SEQUENCE (brief, 2–3 s):** Exterior showing original condition; dark foyer with
poor lighting; cramped kitchen with worn cabinets; dated bathroom fixtures. Fast-paced,
minor-key music. Conveys potential waiting to be unlocked.

**AFTER SEQUENCE — EXTERIOR:** New materials, fresh paint, updated lighting. Property as
premium on street. Slow approach emphasizing curb appeal transformation.

**AFTER — ENTRY AND LIVING:** Foyer now soaring and light-filled. Open-concept living room
with new flooring, updated lighting, fresh paint. Same angle as before shot — transformation
immediately apparent. Slower movement allowing change to sink in.

**AFTER — KITCHEN (headline renovation):** Before flash (0.5 s) then extended after sequence.
Open, modern, abundant light. Detail shots: marble or quartz counters, new appliances, updated
hardware. The transformation is significant.

**AFTER — BATHROOM:** Dated fixtures evolved to luxury spa-like retreat. Quality new materials
and fixtures emphasized.

**FINAL SIDE-BY-SIDES:** Key spaces in before/after sequence — kitchen, master bath, main
living space. Same angle, dramatic contrast.

**EVENING GLOW:** Renovated property at twilight — transformed finishes catch light beautifully.

**FINAL STATEMENT:** Most impressive after space — often kitchen or living room. Music
swells to triumphant crescendo. Overlay: property address, key upgrades, agent contact.

**Music:** Two-part — before section: minor key, sparse, tension. After section: major key,
fuller orchestration, resolution. Transition is clear and satisfying.
**Tone:** Transformative, smart investment, craftsmanship. Viewer spends more time with
the success than the problem.

## Example 5: Vacation Rental — Mountain Retreat

**Model:** `cinematic_studio_3_0`
**Aspect ratio:** `16:9`
**Duration:** `15` s (short-form version)

**HOOK:** Aerial drone approaching property from above surrounding mountains. Dramatic peaks,
forested slopes, property nestled in natural context. Within 2 s, viewer is transported —
escaping to a mountain sanctuary.

**EXTERIOR CONTEXT:** Property's relationship to terrain, landscape, and nearby trails.
Golden hour light emphasizing natural beauty. The setting is as much a character as the property.

**ARRIVAL:** Slow approach to property through landscape. The journey is part of the fantasy
— an escape from the everyday world. Front door opens into space flooded with natural light
and mountain views. Interior: natural wood, stone, earthy palettes.

**PRIMARY LIVING SPACES:** Great room with mountain or forest views and fireplace. Camera
approaches windows slowly; view becomes focal point. Show at different times of day if possible
— morning light, afternoon, golden hour — demonstrating how space transforms.

**KITCHEN AND DINING:** Open-plan with views preserved. Dining positioned to enjoy landscape
while eating. This is where families gather; guests remember meals taken here.

**BEDROOM AND BATHROOMS:** Master bedroom at sunrise — warm golden light and views coming
alive. Bed positioned to take in views. Guest bedrooms shown with warmth and comfort.
Hot tub or soaking tub emphasized as luxury relaxation amenity.

**RECREATION AND ENTERTAINING:** Hot tub on deck at twilight with steam rising. Outdoor fire
pit area as gathering space. Decks and patios positioned for entertaining.

**EVENING AMBIANCE:** Transition to twilight. Fire pit lit. Interior lights glow warmly.
Property as cozy evening refuge and ideal gathering place.

**FINAL STATEMENT:** Signature view — main bedroom window at sunrise, or deck at golden hour.
Long, lingering moment. Music reaches satisfying crescendo. Overlay: availability, nightly rate,
booking link, amenities.

**Music:** Warm, inviting, slightly adventurous — indie folk, acoustic-forward instrumentation.
Tempo relaxed, encouraging viewer to imagine relaxing in this space.
**Tone:** Aspirational escape, adventure, gathering. Sells not just a property but an experience
and a way to spend meaningful time in a beautiful place.

## Virtual Tour vs. Marketing Video

### Marketing Video
**Purpose:** Emotionally engage, tell a story, inspire desire, compel action.
**Characteristics:** Cinematic narrative arc; curated best angles; aspirational; platform-optimized;
designed to be shared.
**Best for:** Premium and luxury properties; social media; initial buzz for listings; agent
brand building.
**Approach:** Professional cinematography with smooth camera movement; narrative arc with music;
optimized for target platform.

### Virtual Tour
**Purpose:** Allow prospective buyers or renters to explore independently at their own pace.
**Characteristics:** Interactive; comprehensive coverage; every room from multiple angles;
functional over emotional; viewer navigates freely.
**Best for:** Initial listing exposure (broad audience); detailed property documentation;
vacation rentals (guests want comprehensive preview); long-term listings.

### Recommended Hybrid Approach (High-Value Properties)
1. **Virtual tour** — comprehensive exploration tool published on MLS, property website, Zillow.
2. **Marketing video** — emotional hook and sharing tool published on YouTube, Instagram, broker
   website, and social media.

The virtual tour serves as reference documentation; the marketing video serves as seduction
and story. Use `cinematic_studio_3_0` for the marketing video clips; use the virtual tour
separately (not AI-generated video).
