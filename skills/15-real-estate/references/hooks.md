# Real Estate Hook Framework

The 2-second hook library for property showcase video prompts. Every real estate video must
hook the viewer in the first two seconds. Use these techniques when building prompts for
`cinematic_studio_3_0` or `veo3_1`.

## The 2-Second Hook Principle

Potential buyers have infinite properties to browse. Your opening must stop them mid-scroll
and plant a question in their mind: "Wait... where is this?"

The hook must:
- Create immediate visual awe, intrigue, or aspiration
- Imply the full property story to come
- Generate a question the viewer wants answered

**Prompt structure:**
```
Opening 0–2 s: [Hook type] — [What appears on screen and the viewer's emotional response]
```

## Hook Techniques

### 1. Drone Aerial Swoop Toward Property
Start high above the property, revealing context: neighborhood, surrounding landscape,
location advantage. Swoop downward and inward toward the front facade or most striking
architectural feature.
- **Trigger:** Awe, perspective, desirability through location.
- **Best for:** Homes with impressive grounds, skyline views, architectural distinction.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Drone begins high above property revealing [neighborhood/landscape context]. Sweeps downward and inward toward [architectural feature/entry] — arriving at eye level by 2 s."

### 2. Door Opening to Reveal Stunning Interior
Shot from inside looking out (or vice versa) — the threshold as the transition between worlds.
Heavy door swings open to reveal soaring ceilings, light-flooded rooms, or a breathtaking view.
- **Trigger:** Anticipation, the promise of luxury within.
- **Best for:** All property types; universally compelling.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Heavy [wooden/glass/steel] door swings open from inside. [Soaring ceilings/light-flooded room/breathtaking view] immediately fills the frame. Threshold crossed by 2 s."

### 3. Window View That Takes Your Breath Away
Pan across a room and find the window — slowly approach until the landscape or cityscape fills
the frame. The view becomes the hero; the window frames aspiration.
- **Trigger:** Envy, wanderlust, lifestyle upgrade.
- **Best for:** Waterfront properties, mountain views, skyline apartments.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Camera pans across [room name]; finds window. Slow approach until [ocean/skyline/landscape] fills the frame completely. View as hero by 2 s."

### 4. Before / After Renovation Flash
Quick cut between the original dilapidated space and the newly renovated showpiece. Light,
contrast, and transformation tell the story of investment value.
- **Trigger:** Potential, transformation, smart investment.
- **Best for:** Renovation properties, fixer-uppers turned luxury.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Flash cut: before condition (harsh, dim, worn) 0–1 s → after condition (bright, pristine, transformed) 1–2 s. Same angle, dramatic contrast."

### 5. Light Flooding Into Dark Space
Begin in shadow; a door or window opening floods the space with golden or bright light.
Materials suddenly come alive; dust motes dance in rays.
- **Trigger:** Warmth, hope, awakening.
- **Best for:** Homes with dramatic skylights, south-facing windows, new construction.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Scene begins in shadow. [Door/window] opens; golden/bright light floods in across [marble/hardwood/tile] floor. Materials come alive instantly. Light fills frame by 2 s."

### 6. Infinity Pool Edge With Landscape Beyond
Shot from water level looking toward the edge; the pool blends with ocean, valley, or cityscape.
Immediately communicates luxury and views.
- **Trigger:** Leisure, opulence, lifestyle aspiration.
- **Best for:** Luxury homes, vacation rentals with premium outdoor spaces.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Camera at water level of infinity pool, looking toward edge. [Ocean/valley/cityscape] visible beyond pool edge — seamless horizon. Luxury immediately evident."

### 7. Architectural Detail Extreme Close-Up
Begin impossibly close to a stunning detail: marble veining, wood joinery, stainless steel
reflection, tile craftsmanship. Pull back to reveal the full room.
- **Trigger:** Craftsmanship, luxury, attention to detail.
- **Best for:** High-end properties, designer homes, notable architecture.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Extreme macro close-up on [marble veining/wood joinery/tile pattern] — texture fills frame. Camera pulls back slowly over 2 s to reveal full [room name]."

### 8. Day-to-Night Transition
Start with property in daylight; time-lapse or seamless transition to twilight and evening.
Interior lights come alive, creating warmth and drama.
- **Trigger:** Versatility, 24-hour lifestyle appeal.
- **Best for:** Urban properties, homes with significant nightlife appeal, luxury residences.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Property in bright daylight (0–1 s). Seamless time-lapse transition to twilight (1–2 s). Interior lights come alive; exterior architecture dramatically illuminated."

### 9. Front Door Approach
Slow, steady approach down a driveway, walkway, or staircase toward the front entrance.
Build anticipation — every step closer is a step toward "home."
- **Trigger:** Welcome, invitation, the feeling of arriving.
- **Best for:** All property types; foundational hook.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Slow, steady approach down [driveway/walkway/staircase] toward front entrance. Each step builds anticipation. Entry door reached but not yet crossed by 2 s."

### 10. Skyline from Penthouse Window
Shot from high above the city; viewer is positioned at window looking out over the urban
landscape. Immediately communicates prestige, height, status, and view.
- **Trigger:** Power, sophistication, exclusivity.
- **Best for:** High-rise apartments, penthouses, urban luxury.
- **Prompt phrase:** "First-person POV at penthouse window looking out over [city] skyline. Height and scale of view communicate prestige immediately. Pull back to reveal interior framing by 2 s."

### 11. Negative Space and Light Play
A room defined more by light and shadow than by objects. Geometric light rectangles move
across floors and walls as camera tracks.
- **Trigger:** Sophistication, minimalism, architectural purity.
- **Best for:** Modern, contemporary, and minimal-design properties.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Camera tracks slowly; geometric light rectangles move across [white walls/polished floors]. Space defined by light and shadow. Minimalist, architectural purity by 2 s."

### 12. Material Transformation Under Light
A surface (marble, wood, glass, concrete) shifts in appearance as light moves across it.
The material itself becomes the visual protagonist.
- **Trigger:** Luxury, tactile beauty, design-forward thinking.
- **Best for:** Properties with statement finishes, high-end materials.
- **Prompt phrase:** "[Marble/wood/concrete] surface in close-up. Light moves slowly across it revealing color depth, texture, and natural variation. Material as protagonist for first 2 s."

## Hook Selection Guide by Property Type

| Property type | Primary hook | Secondary hook |
|---|---|---|
| Luxury waterfront | Drone aerial (1) or infinity pool edge (6) | Window view (3) |
| Urban apartment / penthouse | Skyline window (10) or door reveal (2) | Day-to-night (8) |
| Suburban family home | Front door approach (9) or light flooding (5) | Architectural detail (7) |
| Heritage / period property | Architectural detail close-up (7) | Light flooding (5) |
| New construction | Light flooding (5) or drone aerial (1) | Architectural detail (7) |
| Renovation property | Before / after flash (4) | Light flooding (5) |
| Vacation rental | Infinity pool (6) or window view (3) | Front door approach (9) |
| Land and lot | Drone aerial (1) | Window view (3) |
| Commercial / office | Negative space (11) or door reveal (2) | Light play (11) |

## Hook Stacking

Advanced technique: combine two hook techniques for maximum stopping power.

**Stack 1: Drone + View Reveal**
Drone aerial approach (hook 1) resolves into window view reveal (hook 3). Context from above
leads to the specific compelling view that defines the property.

**Stack 2: Detail + Room Reveal**
Architectural detail close-up (hook 7) pulls back to reveal the full room (hook 2-style reveal).
Craftsmanship establishes quality; room reveal confirms the full picture.

**Stack 3: Light Flood + Walkthrough**
Light flooding into dark space (hook 5) transitions directly into Steadicam walkthrough of
the illuminated space. Drama of discovery leads to exploration.

**Stacking rule:** Maximum two techniques in the first 2 seconds. More creates visual noise.
The goal is a single question planted in the viewer's mind, not a visual catalogue.
