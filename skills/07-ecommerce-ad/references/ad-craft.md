## E-Commerce Ad Philosophy

**1. Hero shots are non-negotiable.** Every e-commerce ad needs a moment where the
product is the star — pure, isolated, with perfect lighting.

**2. Multi-angle storytelling.** Rotate the product. Show front, back, side, detail.
Viewers need to feel they can "hold" the product through the screen.

**3. In-context usage.** Show the product in real life. Not sterile product
photography — genuine lifestyle integration.

**4. Before/after is psychology gold.** Problem state → moment of realisation →
product solution → happy outcome. The journey matters.

**5. Quick-cut energy for scroll stoppers.** Social feeds reward high-energy cuts.
0.5–1 s cuts per shot at peak moments; slower for lifestyle.

**6. Texture and tactile detail.** E-commerce buyers can't touch. Create tactile
desire through close-ups: fabric weave, metal grain, ingredient shine.

**7. Lifestyle aspiration, not just function.** Don't just show what the product
does. Show how it makes the buyer feel — confident, beautiful, organised, powerful.

**8. Text overlays that convert.** Problem identification, key benefit callout,
social proof, pricing and offer, CTA.

## Product Staging Techniques

### Hero Shot (Isolated)
Pure product on clean background with perfect lighting. No distractions. This is
the "money shot" of the ad.
- White or neutral background for most categories
- Dramatic dark background for luxury, tech, jewelry
- Product fills 40–70 % of frame
- At least one close-up of key design detail

### Flat Lay
Product arranged with complementary items or variants from overhead. Camera pulls
back from tight crop to wide reveal, or zooms into individual details.

### Three-quarter View
Product at 45° angle showing depth and dimension. Most effective for objects with
interesting side or back detail.

### On-Body or In-Use
For wearables, beauty, and handheld products: show the product worn or held by a
person. Provides scale and lifestyle context simultaneously.

### Environment / Context
Product placed in its natural habitat (kitchen gadget in kitchen, fitness gear at
gym, furniture in living room). Camera shows product in use within the scene.

## Lighting Playbook

### Key Light (45° angle, warm)
Main illumination. Positions at 45° to product. Warm temperature (2700 K–3500 K)
for lifestyle products; cooler (4000 K–5000 K) for tech and electronics.

### Rim/Back Light
Placed behind product opposite key light. Creates edge glow that separates product
from background. Essential for dark products on dark backgrounds.

### Fill Light or Bounce
Soft secondary source reducing harsh shadows from key. Diffused or bounced off a
white surface. Flattens ugly shadows while maintaining dimension.

### Overhead / Beauty Dish
Even top-down illumination. Common for beauty, jewelry, food. Creates even exposure
without directional shadow.

### Natural Window Light
Soft, diffused, directional. Ideal for lifestyle, fashion, and aspirational product
contexts. Cool-to-neutral temperature. Authentic, approachable.

### Specular Highlights
Directed light creating single-point highlight on reflective surfaces (glass, metal,
gemstones). Controlled glint that signals premium quality.

## Multi-Angle Display Techniques

### 360-Degree Rotation
Smooth continuous rotation showing all sides. Key frames: front-facing, 45° view,
side profile, back detail, top-down. 2–3 full rotations in 8 s.

### Layered Zoom
Start wide → zoom into detail → zoom into macro texture → zoom back out. 3–5 zoom
layers. Reveals product depth and craftsmanship.

### Split-Screen Comparison
Variant A vs variant B side by side. Or before/after state. Consistent centre line.
Text labels each side.

### Multi-Device or Multi-Product Stack
Multiple units arranged and revealed sequentially. Staggered timing creates rhythm
and shows product family.

### Overhead Flat Lay with Zoom
Overhead arrangement of product and accessories; camera zooms into individual product
detail and then back out.

## Lifestyle and Context Integration

### Setting Selection
- **Minimalist / Luxury:** Clean white walls, natural light, isolated spaces
- **Active / Wellness:** Outdoor settings, gyms, nature, bright daylight
- **Cosy / Comfort:** Warm indoor lighting, wooden elements, personal spaces
- **Professional / Tech:** Modern workspace, clean desk, tech-forward aesthetic
- **Social / Celebration:** Group settings, parties, events, vibrant colours

### Proportion Rule
30–40 % of video in lifestyle/in-context shots. 60–70 % product-focused.
Lifestyle section shows product in use; product section shows quality and detail.

### Emotional Arc (15-second structure)
1. Relatable opening — viewer sees themselves in the problem/scenario
2. Product introduction — "here is the solution"
3. Transformation — product makes a difference
4. Aspiration moment — viewer imagines owning and using
5. CTA — clear next step

### Hand Modeling
- Manicured hands for beauty and jewelry
- Active hands for fitness and functional products
- Natural positioning (avoid awkward angles)
- Diversity in hand models where appropriate

## Value Communication Structure

| Video section | What to communicate |
|---|---|
| 0–2 s (hook) | Problem identification or premium signal |
| 2–8 s (showcase) | Key feature or benefit callout; social proof |
| 8–12 s (lifestyle) | Aspiration; show the product integrated in life |
| 12–15 s (CTA) | Price, offer, urgency, next step |

### Social Proof Copy Examples
- "4.9 stars (5,847 reviews)"
- "50K+ customers worldwide"
- "Trending on TikTok"
- "Award-winning design"
- "Viral bestseller"

## Text and CTA Guide

### Strategic Text Placement

**0–2 s hook text:** Problem statement or attention-grab phrase. Large, bold,
readable on mobile. High contrast. Fade-in or pop effect.

**Product showcase (middle) text:** Key benefit (Waterproof, All-Day Wear,
Eco-Friendly). Social proof (2,000+ Reviews, Sold Out In 48 Hours). Feature callout
(Made in Italy, Dermatologist Tested).

**Final 3 s CTA text:** Price if not previously shown. Discount or offer (30% OFF,
Free Shipping, Limited Time). CTA button text (Shop Now, Get It Today, Learn More).
Urgency marker (Only 12 Left, Sale Ends Sunday).

### CTA Animation Styles
- **Slide-in:** Text slides from left/right with bounce
- **Pop:** Text appears with scale effect and slight shake
- **Glow:** Text pulses to draw the eye
- **Button effect:** Text sits in clickable button shape with interaction state

### Font and Readability Rules
- Sans-serif for maximum mobile readability
- Minimum 24 pt for mobile viewing
- Max 8 words per text overlay
- High contrast ratio (WCAG AA minimum)
- Bold is better than thin for overlays

## Quick-Cut Patterns for Social

### Pattern 1: Hook → Showcase → Benefit → CTA (15 seconds)
- 0–2 s: Hook
- 2–8 s: Product showcase (multiple angles, 2–3 cuts)
- 8–12 s: Lifestyle / benefit moment
- 12–15 s: CTA with urgency

### Pattern 2: Before/After Sandwich (12 seconds)
- 0–3 s: Before state (problem)
- 3–9 s: Product showcase
- 9–12 s: After state (solution) with CTA

### Pattern 3: Rapid Variant Carousel (15 seconds)
- 0–2 s: Hook showing first variant
- 2–13 s: Quick cuts cycling through variants (1–2 s each)
- 13–15 s: Best-selling variant highlight with CTA

### Pattern 4: Unboxing Journey (15 seconds)
- 0–2 s: Sealed box reveal
- 2–5 s: Opening box
- 5–12 s: Product reveal and rotation
- 12–15 s: CTA

### Pattern 5: Testimonial Embed (15 seconds)
- 0–1 s: Hook
- 1–8 s: Product showcase
- 8–13 s: Creator or customer reaction
- 13–15 s: CTA

## Sound Design for Product Ads

### ASMR and Tactile Sounds
Pair with product-appropriate sounds:
- Fabric rustling (clothing unfolding)
- Paper crinkling (unboxing)
- Product click/snap (jewelry box, gadget)
- Pouring (beverages, beauty products)
- Buttons clicking or pressing
- Whoosh transitions between cuts

### Music Selection by Category
- **Upbeat and energetic:** Fashion, electronics, fast-paced social (120–140 BPM)
- **Luxe and minimal:** Beauty, jewelry, high-end products (80–100 BPM)
- **Warm and cosy:** Home, wellness, comfort products (90–110 BPM)
- **Trend-forward:** TikTok-style ads, trending audio

### Volume Layering
- ASMR sound effect: 0–30 % volume
- Background music: 50–70 % volume
- Voiceover (if used): 80–100 % volume
- Ambient background: 20–40 % volume

## Product Category Playbook

### Fashion and Apparel
Key visuals: flat lay with texture detail, on-body fit from multiple angles, movement
sequence, fabric drape and flow, colour variant cascade.
Hook: Colour Cascade or In-Hand Usage.
Lifestyle: model in natural setting. Text: size range, material, fit guarantee, price.

### Beauty and Skincare
Key visuals: product bottle with light refraction, ingredient macro shots, before/after
skin transformation, application demonstration, skin glow enhancement.
Hook: Satisfying Texture, Before/After, or Ingredient Explosion.
Text: key ingredients, benefits, SPF/actives, price, "clinically proven."

### Electronics and Gadgets
Key visuals: unboxing reveal, multi-angle design showcase, functional demonstration,
size comparison in hand, durability or feature showcase.
Hook: Product Drop or Unboxing Reveal.
Text: specs (battery, storage), unique feature, warranty, preorder.

### Jewelry and Accessories
Key visuals: macro of craftsmanship, 360 rotation, on-body wearing, light interaction
with gemstones/metals, scale with hand.
Hook: Satisfying Texture or Product Drop.
Text: material (14K gold, lab-grown diamond), dimensions, certification, price.

### Home and Furniture
Key visuals: room setting with product in context, material quality close-up, feature
demonstration, multi-angle views, scale comparison.
Hook: Lifestyle Aspiration or Before/After.
Text: dimensions, materials, assembly time, colour options, price.

### Fitness and Wellness
Key visuals: product in active use, fabric/material technology close-up, before/after,
comfort and fit demonstration, colour variants.
Hook: Before/After or Problem-Solution Snap.
Text: key tech feature, size range, price, warranty, customer rating.

## Common Mistakes and Fixes

**Unclear product showcase:** Use isolated hero shots with 3+ clear angles and
proper lighting. White background always works for e-commerce.

**Hook doesn't stop the scroll:** Choose a specific hook framework; make it visual,
not text-based; add movement, colour contrast, or emotion. Ask: would YOU stop?

**Too much information:** One key message per 5 s. Max 5–8 words per overlay.
Benefit > feature > price. Let visuals do 70 % of the work.

**Lifestyle context missing:** 30–40 % of video should show product in use context.
Show person wearing, using, or enjoying it in a relatable setting.

**Weak or missing CTA:** Final 3 s must have a clear CTA. Action words: Shop Now,
Get It Today, Join Waitlist. Include price if not shown earlier. Add urgency.

**Wrong music choice:** Match BPM to energy level and product category. Luxury
products: 80–100 BPM. Social products: 130–150 BPM. Ensure commercial licence.

**Cuts too fast or too slow:** Social media: 0.75–1.5 s cuts. YouTube: 2–3 s holds.
Match cuts to music beat. Quick-cut at hook; slow for lifestyle; quick for CTA.

**Product not premium-looking:** Specify dramatic lighting in prompts: "dramatic side
lighting", "rim lighting to separate from background", "key light from top-left".
Use colour grading: warm for luxury, cool for tech, vibrant for youth products.
