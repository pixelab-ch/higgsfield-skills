# Comic-to-Video Hook Framework

The first 2 seconds of a comic-to-video animation must capture the viewer's attention and
establish the visual grammar for the entire sequence. This reference documents the 2-second
hook framework and ten essential hook techniques.

## Why the 2-Second Hook Matters

Comic-to-video animations compete for attention in the same feed as native video. The first
2 seconds must:

- Establish the visual grammar (manga speed-lines, Western heavy-ink, webtoon flatness)
- Signal the tone (action, comedy, emotional, horror)
- Create immediate engagement that prevents a scroll-away

Hooks are especially critical for comic content because the audience needs to understand
they are watching animation, not a static scan of a panel — the hook makes the transition
from "image" to "motion" explicit.

## Ten Essential Comic-to-Video Hooks

### 1. Dramatic Panel Crack / Shatter Reveal

The panel border cracks and shatters, revealing the animated scene within. Used for action
climaxes, shocking revelations, or dynamic scene transitions.

**Best for:** Action panels, plot reveals, fight-sequence openers
**Prompt phrasing:** "Panel border CRACKS from [origin point], shattering outward. Crack pattern
radiates from [impact location]. Scene is revealed through the fractures in 2 seconds."

### 2. Speech Bubble Pops to Life

A character's dialogue bubble expands and bursts into visibility, pulling attention to the
speaker. The character's mouth and gestures animate in sync.

**Best for:** Dramatic declarations, urgent warnings, comedic punchlines
**Prompt phrasing:** "Speech bubble trembles at [location], then expands with a burst. Character's
mouth begins to animate as the bubble reaches full size at 1.5 s."

### 3. Ink Splash Transition

An ink explosion erupts across the frame, temporarily obscuring the current panel and
revealing a new scene. The splash acts as a morphing transition.

**Best for:** Scene changes with artistic emphasis, climactic moments
**Prompt phrasing:** "Ink explosion erupts from [character/center/edge], spreads across frame,
obscures [previous content], reveals [next scene] behind it by 2 s."

### 4. Page Turn Reveal

The current panel folds or curls like a page turning, revealing the next scene beneath.
Creates a tactile, book-like transition.

**Best for:** Webtoons, manga, medium-aware transitions between sequences
**Prompt phrasing:** "Panel folds / curls from [corner/edge], peeling back to reveal the animated
scene underneath. Turn completes at 1.8 s."

### 5. Panel Borders Dissolve

The rigid grid structure of panel borders softens and dissolves, allowing character motion
to break free from the static frame.

**Best for:** Transitions from stillness to motion, emotional breakthroughs
**Prompt phrasing:** "Panel borders soften and fade over 1.5 s. Character motion extends beyond
where the border was, breaking into unframed space."

### 6. Character Steps Out of Frame

A foreground character takes a step or gesture that moves them partially or fully out of the
original panel border into three-dimensional space.

**Best for:** Action openers, power reveals, dramatic entrances
**Prompt phrasing:** "Character's [limb/body] moves past the panel border at 0.8 s. Depth cues
extend the movement naturally into space beyond the frame."

### 7. Speed Lines Become Motion Blur

Manga-style speed lines animate into full motion blur. The 2D motion indicators transform
into 3D camera movement and character dynamics.

**Best for:** Action manga, fight-sequence openers, speed/velocity emphasis
**Prompt phrasing:** "Speed lines radiating from [origin] animate into directional motion blur at
[velocity]. Character/object begins moving in the direction indicated by the lines."

### 8. Spotlight or Light Flare Focus

A light effect (lens flare, spotlight, magical glow) sweeps across the panel, drawing
attention and triggering character animation where it lands.

**Best for:** Dramatic pacing, focus-guiding, thriller and mystery panels
**Prompt phrasing:** "Spotlight sweeps across frame from [start] to [end] over 2 s. Character
animation activates where the light lands at [timestamp]."

### 9. Thought Bubble Unfolds into Background

A character's thought or dream bubble expands to become the full scene, visualizing their
internal experience as a narrative depth transition.

**Best for:** Flashbacks, daydream sequences, introspective moments
**Prompt phrasing:** "Thought bubble at [location] expands over 2 s, its borders stretching to
fill the full frame, dissolving into the imagined scene behind."

### 10. Background Comes Alive

While the character remains relatively static, environmental elements (fire, water, wind,
crowds, machinery) animate within the scene.

**Best for:** Establishing atmosphere, environmental storytelling, dramatic stillness
**Prompt phrasing:** "Character holds static pose. Environment animates: [element 1] [motion],
[element 2] [motion]. Duration: 2 s establishing motion."

## Hook Selection Guide

| Content type | Recommended hooks |
|-------------|------------------|
| Action / combat sequences | Panel Crack/Shatter (#1), Speed Lines → Motion Blur (#7) |
| Dialogue / dramatic beats | Speech Bubble Pops (#2), Spotlight Focus (#8) |
| Emotional transitions | Panel Borders Dissolve (#5), Thought Bubble Unfolds (#9) |
| Environmental storytelling | Background Comes Alive (#10), Page Turn Reveal (#4) |
| Comedic timing | Character Steps Out (#6), Speech Bubble Pops (#2) |
| Webtoon / vertical format | Page Turn (#4), Spotlight (#8) |
| Manga action | Speed Lines (#7), Panel Crack (#1) |
| Single illustration | Ink Splash (#3), Background Comes Alive (#10) |
