## Fashion Video Philosophy

### Movement Sells Clothes
Clothing looks alive when the model moves. A stationary pose is lifeless; a walk, sway,
or gesture transforms a simple outfit into a story. Describe how the fabric should move:
"silk blouse catches light and gently ripples" creates desire; "static blouse" does not.

### Fabric in Motion
Silk ripples. Denim has weight and structure. Chiffon floats. Leather creases distinctly.
Your prompt must describe how the fabric behaves in motion.

### Lighting Flatters the Wearer
Fashion lives and dies by light. Golden hour is universally flattering. Studio flash creates
editorial crispness. Neon creates edge. Shadows create drama. Always specify lighting because
it changes how the garment reads on camera.

### Environment as Mood
A white minimalist studio says luxury and minimalism. A gritty urban street says streetwear
and rebellion. A lush garden says romantic and aspirational. The environment is context that
sells the aesthetic.

### The Model as Character
Direct the model's energy, emotions, and attitude. "Confident strut" vs. "uncertain walk"
fundamentally changes how viewers perceive the outfit.

### Rhythm of Outfit Changes
Fast cuts create energy. Slow transitions create luxury. A 10-second lookbook should flow
like music: intro, build, climax, resolution.

### Balance Aspiration with Relatability
Haute couture aspires; everyday wear relates. Find the sweet spot between unattainable and
accessible — the viewer should be able to imagine themselves in the outfit.

## Model Direction Keywords

### Walk Types
- **Power walk:** Confident, direct stride toward camera, shoulders back, purposeful.
- **Slow sway:** Gentle side-to-side movement, fluid, relaxed, sensual.
- **Strut:** Attitude-forward walk with swagger, exaggerated confidence, head high.
- **Catwalk / runway:** Straight line, deliberate heel-to-toe placement, poised, dramatic.
- **Casual amble:** Relaxed walk, natural gait, approachable, everyday.
- **Float / glide:** Ethereal, effortless movement, minimal effort visible.
- **Stomp / bold stride:** Heavy, confident footfalls, strong presence, attitude.

### Pose Types
- **Standing profile:** Side angle showcases silhouette and drape.
- **Three-quarter turn:** Partial rotation shows front and side simultaneously.
- **Hands on hips:** Confident, powerful, statement-making.
- **Hands in pockets:** Casual, approachable, relaxed.
- **Over-shoulder glance:** Confident, mysterious, editorial.
- **Full rotation / spin:** Showcases all angles, playful, dynamic.
- **Lean or recline:** Relaxed, sensual, editorial, languid.

### Expression Types
- **Strong gaze:** Direct eye contact, confidence, intensity.
- **Subtle smile:** Approachable, warm, friendly, relatable.
- **Smoldering look:** Sensual, confident, editorial, attitude.
- **Blank slate:** High-fashion, editorial, lets outfit speak.
- **Genuine laugh:** Authentic joy, playful, relatability.
- **Contemplative gaze:** Thoughtful, editorial, moody.
- **Attitude / side-eye:** Attitude-forward, streetwear, rebellious energy.

### Attitude Keywords
- Confidence, sensuality, playfulness, attitude, grace, approachability, edginess, rebelliousness.

### Hand and Arm Gestures
- Arms relaxed at sides, hands through hair, adjusting garment, pointing, crossed arms,
  arms overhead, hand on chest.

## Fabric and Material Showcase

### Silk and Satin
Lustrous, reflective, drapes gracefully, catches light.
**Prompt:** "Silk fabric catches light and shimmers with each movement. Gentle wind causes
fabric to flow smoothly. Ripples and folds create subtle light reflections."
**Best movement:** Slow walks, gentle sways, wind effects, flowing transitions.
**Lighting:** Directional light highlighting sheen; golden hour or studio flash.

### Denim and Cotton
Textured, matte, structured, fades with wear.
**Prompt:** "Denim shows natural wrinkles and creases as model walks. Fabric has weight and
structure. Worn patches or fade details visible."
**Best movement:** Natural walks, dynamic movement, sits and stands showing creases.
**Lighting:** Natural daylight, bright and even.

### Leather and Suede
Smooth and glossy (leather) or soft and matte (suede), creases distinctly.
**Prompt:** "Leather jacket moves with the body, creasing naturally at elbows and waist.
Glossy sheen catches light as model walks."
**Best movement:** Confident walks, arm movements to show jacket drape.
**Lighting:** Studio flash or bright light; creates defined shadows.

### Chiffon and Lightweight Fabrics
Flowing, delicate, catches air easily, ethereal.
**Prompt:** "Chiffon flows and floats around the body. Even subtle air movement causes fabric
to billow gently. Translucent quality allows light through."
**Best movement:** Slow walks, swaying, wind effects, spins.
**Lighting:** Backlight or sidelighting to show translucence.

### Knit and Sweater Fabric
Textured, warm, stretches and clings slightly, shows ribbing and weave detail.
**Prompt:** "Knit sweater hugs the body subtly, showing natural fabric texture. Ribbing and
weave detail visible in close-up. Fabric stretches naturally with movement."
**Best movement:** Natural walks, arm movements showing stretch.

### Velvet and Crushed Fabrics
Luxurious, color-shifting with nap direction, dramatic light play.
**Prompt:** "Velvet shows color depth and subtle sheen as model moves. Nap direction creates
shifting highlights. Light plays across surface creating depth."
**Best movement:** Rotations and turns to show light play, sensual slow movements.

### Sequins and Beaded Fabric
Reflective, glittery, catches and throws light.
**Prompt:** "Sequin dress throws light and sparkles with every movement. Close-up reveals
individual sequins catching light. Rotations create cascading light effects."
**Best movement:** Spins, rotations, dynamic movement to maximize light reflection.
**Lighting:** Dramatic studio or spotlighting.

### Lace and Sheer Overlays
Delicate, intricate patterning, translucent, layered.
**Prompt:** "Lace overlay shows intricate pattern details. Sheer sections reveal garment
underneath. Delicate, romantic aesthetic."
**Best movement:** Slow sensual walks, hand movements to reveal lace pattern, spins.
**Lighting:** Backlighting or sidelighting to show sheer areas.

### Technical and Performance Fabrics
Seamless, engineered, minimal bulk, modern.
**Prompt:** "Technical fabric looks modern and engineered. Seamless construction visible.
Fabric stretches and moves with body without excess material."
**Best movement:** Athletic movement, stretching, dynamic action.

## Lighting for Fashion

### Editorial Lighting (Studio Flash)
Crisp, high-contrast, shadow definition, professional polish.
**Best for:** Haute couture, luxury brands, professional campaigns.
**Prompt:** "Studio lighting with crisp directional flash. Clean shadows define garment
silhouette. Professional editorial aesthetic."

### Golden Hour (Warm, Diffused Sun)
Warm, flattering, romantic, universally beautiful, soft shadows.
**Best for:** All categories, especially casual wear, swimwear, romantic fashion.
**Prompt:** "Golden hour lighting bathes model in warm, flattering light. Soft shadows create
dimension. Warm orange and amber tones complement most skin tones and colors."

### Bright Daylight (Midday Sun)
High-contrast, harsh shadows, bright and energetic.
**Best for:** Streetwear, activewear, casual wear, summery content.
**Prompt:** "Bright natural daylight creates clear, energetic look. Colors pop with clarity.
Energetic, summery aesthetic."

### Neon and Night Lighting
Artificial, edgy, colorful, dramatic, modern.
**Best for:** Streetwear, contemporary fashion, evening wear.
**Prompt:** "Neon signs cast colored light on model. Urban nighttime setting. High-contrast
artificial light creates dramatic effect."

### Natural Window Light (Soft and Diffused)
Soft, directional, flattering, intimate.
**Best for:** Everyday wear, casual fashion, sustainable fashion.
**Prompt:** "Soft window light creates gentle directional illumination. No harsh shadows.
Intimate, approachable aesthetic."

### Dramatic Shadows and Backlighting
High-contrast, silhouette focus, moody, artistic.
**Best for:** Luxury, evening wear, artistic campaigns.
**Prompt:** "Backlighting creates dramatic silhouette. Silhouette of garment is emphasized.
Moody, artistic, high-fashion aesthetic."

### Colorized / Gel Lighting
Fashion-forward, trendy, themed, artistic.
**Best for:** Contemporary fashion, editorial shoots.
**Prompt:** "Blue gel lighting casts cool tone across scene. Model skin and garment take on
color-shifted quality. Artistic, contemporary, fashion-forward mood."

## Location and Setting Library

### Studio (White, Black, or Colored Backdrop)
Clean, professional, fashion-forward, undistracted.
**Best for:** Haute couture, professional lookbooks, luxury brands, accessory focus.
**Prompt:** "Pure white studio backdrop. Model stands out clearly. No environmental
distractions. Professional, high-fashion aesthetic."

### Urban Street
Streetwear, contemporary, gritty, authentic, edgy.
**Prompt:** "Urban street setting with brick walls, graffiti, sidewalk. Natural urban textures.
Gritty, authentic energy."

### Nature and Garden
Romantic, organic, sustainable, peaceful, whimsical.
**Prompt:** "Model in lush garden setting. Green foliage background. Natural flowers and plants
frame shot. Romantic, organic aesthetic."

### Beach and Ocean
Summer, carefree, adventurous, tropical, sensual.
**Prompt:** "Sandy beach with ocean waves. Golden sand, blue water background. Tropical,
summer aesthetic."

### Rooftop / Urban Skyline
Luxury, urban sophistication, modern, aspirational.
**Prompt:** "Rooftop with city skyline backdrop. Golden hour light. Sophisticated,
aspirational urban aesthetic."

### Industrial / Warehouse
Edgy, contemporary, alternative, moody, rebellious.
**Prompt:** "Industrial warehouse with concrete walls, metal beams. Raw, unfinished aesthetic.
Moody lighting through windows."

## Outfit Change Transitions

### Flash Cut
Instant cut between outfit angles (0.3–0.5 s per outfit). **Best for:** Multi-look lookbooks.

### Spin Transition
Model spins; outfit changes mid-rotation. **Best for:** Playful campaigns, multi-look.

### Door / Curtain Walk-Through
Model walks through door or curtain; emerges in new outfit. **Best for:** Reveals, elegant.

### Mirror Transition
Model's reflection changes in mirror; camera pans to reveal new outfit. **Best for:** Editorial.

### Shadow Silhouette Transition
Model's shadow shifts; real model enters in new outfit. **Best for:** Artistic, contemporary.

### Zoom In / Out Transition
Rapid zoom to detail (shoe, bag); pulls back to reveal new outfit. **Best for:** Accessory-focused.

### Fade or Dissolve
First outfit fades to black; second fades in. **Best for:** Elegant, formal, polished.

### Light Shift / Color Grade Change
Lighting or color temperature shifts; outfit changes during shift. **Best for:** Mood-driven.

## Fashion Category Guide

### Haute Couture and Luxury Fashion
Emphasize craftsmanship, fabric movement, luxe materials.
**Keywords:** Dramatic silhouette, flowing fabric, jewel tones, editorial lighting, slow elegant
movements, intricate detailing, exclusive, rare materials, bespoke fit.
**Model direction:** Grace, poise, understated confidence, slow deliberate movements.
**Hook:** Slow fabric wind reveal, silhouette against minimal background, macro texture close-up.

### Streetwear and Urban Fashion
Fast cuts, attitude, urban environment, dynamic movement.
**Keywords:** Bold colors, graphic prints, oversized fit, sneaker focus, attitude-driven,
street energy, quick cuts, confident swagger.
**Hook:** Power walk at camera, rapid outfit changes, silhouette spin.

### Athleisure and Activewear
Movement and motion — show how fabric stretches, moves, performs.
**Keywords:** High-tech fabrics, seamless design, flexibility, range of motion, dynamic movement.
**Hook:** Model in mid-motion (running, jumping, stretching), rapid movement cuts.

### Formal and Business Fashion
Clean lines, precision tailoring, confident movement, professional environments.
**Keywords:** Structured silhouette, tailored fit, professional polish, minimalist elegance.
**Hook:** Model descends stairs in suit, mirror reveal of polished silhouette, power walk.

### Casual and Everyday Wear
Authenticity, relatability, genuine comfort.
**Keywords:** Comfortable fit, effortless style, accessible, relatable, casual grace, genuine.
**Hook:** Model approach camera in casual walk, candid moment, genuine smile.

### Swimwear and Beachwear
Body confidence, movement in water/sand, vibrant colors.
**Keywords:** Bold colors, body-confident pose, beach setting, water elements, confidence.
**Hook:** Model walks from ocean, slow-mo water droplets, confident beach strut.

### Accessories Focus (Shoes, Bags, Jewelry)
Macro detail shots, zoom reveals, close-up craftsmanship.
**Keywords:** Intricate detailing, craftsmanship, zoom reveal, luxury materials, tactile surface.
**Hook:** Macro shot of shoe leather, bag zipper, jewelry sparkle; zoom out to full look.

### Evening and Party Wear
Drama, movement in fabric, confidence, celebration energy.
**Keywords:** Glamorous silhouette, dramatic color, statement piece, sequins, evening elegance.
**Hook:** Curtain reveal of dramatic dress, spin in flowing fabric, slow-mo fabric billow.

## Multi-Look Efficiency

When showcasing 5+ outfits in a short clip, pacing and transitions are critical.

**Fast-paced lookbook structure (12 s max on cinematic_studio_video_v2):**
- **0–2 s:** 2-second hook (stopping power)
- **2–5 s:** Outfit 1 (walk, turn, 1–2 key angles)
- **5–7 s:** Flash cut or spin transition to Outfit 2
- **7–10 s:** Outfit 2 (quick showcase, minimal)
- **10–12 s:** Final pose in Outfit 3 or ensemble shot

**Transition tactics for speed:**
1. Flash cuts: 0.3–0.5 s per outfit; instant, no transition effect.
2. Spin transitions: model spins off-camera, re-enters in new outfit.
3. Rapid zoom cuts: zoom in on detail (shoe, bag), pull back to reveal next outfit.
4. Light shifts: lighting change covers outfit swap.
5. Environment change cuts: studio to outdoor masks outfit change.

## Platform Optimization

### Instagram Reels / TikTok (9:16)
- Vertical format, music-first platform, trending audio.
- First 1 second must stop the scroll.
- Text overlays guide narrative.
- Fast cuts, quick pacing, high visual polish.

### YouTube Shorts (9:16 or 16:9)
- Slightly more patient audience; up to 12 s allows full arc.
- Thumbnails are critical.
- Narrative development rewarded.

### Instagram Feed (1:1)
- Center-weighted composition.
- Colors must be saturated for feed performance.
- Hook must capture attention in first 0.5 s (auto-play, often muted).
- Text overlay critical.

### YouTube Standard (16:9)
- Longer-form narrative is viable within the 12 s cap.
- SEO and title/description matter.
- Opening frame is compelling as standalone thumbnail.

## Common Mistakes and Fixes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Static model, boring outfit | Add deliberate movement: walk, sway, turn, gesture. Show how fabric flows. |
| Unclear garment details | Be hyperspecific: color name, fabric type, fit details, accessories, shoes, hair. |
| Vague lighting | Specify direction, quality (hard vs. soft), and how light interacts with fabric. |
| Weak hook | Use proven hooks: dramatic reveals, close-ups of detail, surprising angle, motion. |
| Outfit doesn't match brand aesthetic | Clarify brand vibe; every detail must align with brand identity. |
| Vague model direction | Use specific keywords: confident strut, slow sway, playful spin, introspective gaze. |
| Too many outfits, not enough time | Extend duration or reduce outfit count; each outfit needs ≥3 s minimum. |
| Weak transitions | Use seamless techniques: spin masks, light shifts, environment changes, zoom cuts. |
| Accessories ignored | Deliberately highlight with close-ups, specific angles, deliberate movement. |
| No clear call to action | Include integrated CTA with text overlay, website, or collection name. |
