# Fashion Lookbook Hook Framework

The 2-second hook library for fashion video prompts. Every great fashion video captures
attention in the first 2 seconds. Use these techniques when building prompts for
`cinematic_studio_video_v2` or `seedance_2_0`.

## The 2-Second Hook Principle

Your opening shot is everything. Viewers decide in the first 2 seconds whether to keep
watching or scroll past. Every fashion prompt must begin with a deliberate hook that:
- Stops the scroll immediately
- Implies the full story to come
- Creates a visual question the viewer wants answered

The hook is separate from the main content. It is the entry door. After the hook, the rest
of the video earns its time.

**Prompt structure for the hook:**
```
Opening 0–2 seconds: [Hook type] — [What appears on screen and how it creates intrigue]
```

## Hook Techniques

### 1. Dramatic Outfit Reveal (Curtain / Door)
Model stands behind sheer curtain or walks through door. Curtain pulls back or door swings
open. Outfit is fully revealed; camera holds for 0.5 seconds.
- **Trigger:** Anticipation, instant impact, full outfit visible.
- **Best for:** Haute couture, launch reveals, formal wear.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Sheer curtain pulls back slowly, revealing model in full [outfit description]. Camera holds for 0.5 seconds on complete silhouette."

### 2. Model Power-Walk Directly at Camera
Model walks straight toward camera at moderate pace, confidence radiating. Camera slightly
tilts down as they approach; model dominates frame by second 1.5.
- **Trigger:** Attitude and confidence.
- **Best for:** Streetwear, athleisure, bold campaigns.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Model walks directly toward camera at confident pace, shoulders back. By 1.5 s, model fills frame and attitude is fully established."

### 3. Rapid Outfit Change Flash Cuts
Cut between 2–4 different outfit angles in 1 second. Each outfit appears for 0.3–0.5 seconds.
- **Trigger:** Variety, excitement, showcases collection.
- **Best for:** Lookbooks, multi-piece collections, style guides.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Rapid flash cuts between [N] outfit angles — each lasting 0.3–0.5 s. Dizzying, exciting pace that establishes variety before hook resolves."

### 4. Fabric Texture Extreme Macro
Camera starts extreme close-up on fabric — weave, sequins, leather texture visible. Slowly
pulls back to reveal model wearing the piece.
- **Trigger:** Tactile, aspirational, craftsmanship.
- **Best for:** Luxury materials, sustainable fabrics, fabric-forward campaigns.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Extreme macro close-up on [fabric type] — individual [weave/sequins/texture] visible filling frame. Camera pulls back slowly over 2 s to reveal model wearing the piece."

### 5. Slow-Motion Hair and Fabric Wind
Model stationary or walking. Hair and fabric billow in slow-motion wind. Sensual, dramatic,
fashion-film energy.
- **Trigger:** Luxury, romance, editorial.
- **Best for:** Evening wear, formal wear, luxury brands.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Model's hair and [fabric type] billow in gentle slow-motion wind. Camera holds on movement. Sensual, fashion-film energy in first 2 s."

### 6. Mirror or Reflection Reveal
Model's reflection appears in a mirror, window, or reflective surface before full body is seen.
Turn, tilt, or approach reveals outfit.
- **Trigger:** Elegant, editorial, slightly mysterious.
- **Best for:** Formal wear, accessories, beauty-integrated campaigns.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Camera finds model's reflection in [mirror/window/reflective surface] first. Model turns toward camera, reflection shifting. By 2 s, full outfit visible in reflection."

### 7. Color-Coordinated Environment Match
Model outfit matches or complements background. Monochromatic harmony draws eye to silhouette.
- **Trigger:** Visual harmony, editorial, minimalist.
- **Best for:** Minimalist fashion, editorial, mood-driven content.
- **Prompt phrase:** "[Outfit color] outfit against [matching/complementary background color] background. Color harmony immediately draws eye to model silhouette."

### 8. Accessory Close-Up to Full Look
Open on detailed shot of shoes, bag, or jewelry. Camera pulls back to show full model and outfit.
Accessory stars first, then context.
- **Trigger:** Craftsmanship, luxury, accessory-forward.
- **Best for:** Accessory campaigns, luxury details, jewelry.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Extreme close-up on [shoes/bag/jewelry] — craftsmanship detail filling frame. Camera pulls back over 2 s to reveal full model and complete look."

### 9. Silhouette Shadow Spin
Model's shadow on wall or ground. Silhouette spins, moves, reveals outfit shape. Real model
enters frame.
- **Trigger:** Mysterious, playful, modern.
- **Best for:** Contemporary fashion, streetwear, trendy brands.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Model's shadow on [wall/ground] shows spinning silhouette. Shadow communicates outfit shape before model enters frame in 2 s. Playful, artistic."

### 10. Elevator or Descent Reveal
Model descends stairs, steps out of elevator, or walks down ramp. Camera frames from front
or side. Movement conveys confidence; outfit reveals progressively.
- **Trigger:** Luxury, formal, powerful.
- **Best for:** Luxury, formal wear, power fashion.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Model descends [stairs/elevator/ramp] from above. Progressive reveal: legs first, then torso, then face. Each step builds anticipation."

### 11. Zoom or Scale Shift
Camera rapidly zooms from wide shot (full outfit/environment) to medium (torso/face), or
vice versa. Creates energy and emphasizes key pieces.
- **Trigger:** Energy, focus, emphasis.
- **Best for:** Detail-oriented lookbooks, accessory showcases, multi-piece collections.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Rapid camera zoom from [wide/medium] to [detail/wide]. Scale shift happens in 1.5 s, creating immediate energy and directing focus."

### 12. Rotation Spin (360° or Partial)
Model rotates slowly (360°) or spins (180°–270°). Camera circles or stays static. Showcases
outfit from all angles.
- **Trigger:** Full-look reveal, confidence, tailoring-focused.
- **Best for:** Fit showcases, tailoring-focused campaigns, collection pieces.
- **Prompt phrase:** "Model begins slow [360°/partial] rotation from frame entry. Camera stays static; outfit revealed from all angles as rotation completes by 2 s."

## Hook Stacking

Advanced technique: combine two hook techniques for maximum stopping power.

**Stack 1: Macro + Reveal**
Open on macro fabric texture (hook 4), then immediately cut to power walk at camera (hook 2).
The macro establishes craftsmanship; the power walk establishes attitude.

**Stack 2: Shadow + Spin**
Silhouette shadow spin (hook 9) resolves into the actual model performing a 360° rotation (hook 12).
Mystery resolves into full-look showcase.

**Stack 3: Flash Cuts + Zoom**
Rapid flash cuts between looks (hook 3) end with a zoom into a single hero look (hook 11).
Variety followed by focus.

**Stacking rule:** Maximum two techniques in the first 2 seconds. More than two creates
visual noise and loses the viewer instead of grabbing them.

## Hook Selection Guide

| Fashion category | Primary hook | Secondary hook |
|-----------------|-------------|----------------|
| Haute couture / luxury | Fabric macro (4) or curtain reveal (1) | Slow-mo wind (5) |
| Streetwear / urban | Power walk (2) | Flash cuts (3) or shadow spin (9) |
| Athleisure / activewear | Mid-motion model (power walk 2) | Scale shift zoom (11) |
| Formal / evening wear | Descent reveal (10) or curtain reveal (1) | Slow-mo wind (5) or mirror (6) |
| Casual / everyday | Casual power walk (2) | Color-match environment (7) |
| Accessories focus | Accessory close-up (8) | Macro texture (4) |
| Multi-look lookbook | Flash cuts (3) | Spin rotation (12) |
| Sustainable / artisanal | Macro fabric (4) | Color-match environment (7) |
