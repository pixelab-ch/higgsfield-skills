## Platform-Specific Optimization

### TikTok Hook Optimization

TikTok's algorithm is most aggressive about the first 3 seconds. TikTok users are the
fastest scrollers.

**TikTok-specific requirements:**
- Vertical format non-negotiable (9:16)
- Text overlays are EXPECTED (use them liberally)
- Audio is paramount (trending audio gets algorithmic boost)
- Hook must be copy-pasteable into TikTok's editing interface
- Hashtag strategy matters (#ForYouPage, #FYP, trending hashtags)

**Prompt template for TikTok:**
Create a TikTok hook video prompt that opens with [VISUAL SHOCK] at 0.2 seconds. At 0.5
seconds, add on-screen text in CapCut-friendly format: "[LARGE TEXT HOOK]". From 0.5–1.2
seconds, show [CURIOSITY BUILD]. At 1.2 seconds, add second text: "[SECONDARY TEXT]".
From 1.2–2.0 seconds, show [MOMENTUM]. Audio: [TRENDING AUDIO MATCHING HOOK TYPE] or
[ORIGINAL SOUND DESCRIPTION]. The hook must stop the infinite TikTok scroll. Format
output as video description, then editing checklist for CapCut or TikTok's native editor.

**TikTok hook-specific elements:**
- Text on screen should appear with motion (not static)
- Font: bold, sans-serif, high contrast
- First text at 0.5 s, second at 1.2 s (proven timing)
- Use trending audio of the moment (algorithm boost is real)
- Hashtag must include #FYP or #ForYouPage for discovery
- 15–60 seconds total video is optimal for TikTok

### Instagram Reels Hook Optimization

Instagram Reels audience is slightly older, values polish and aesthetics. Reels algorithm
rewards saved videos and shares (not just watch-through).

**Instagram Reels-specific requirements:**
- Vertical format (9:16)
- Aesthetic consistency (color palette, lighting, vibe)
- Branded if from a creator/business (consistency with feed)
- Hook + satisfying resolution (Reels viewers want completion)
- Caption matters for context

**Prompt template for Instagram Reels:**
Create an Instagram Reels hook optimized for save/share rate. Open with [VISUALLY
POLISHED SHOCK]. First 2 seconds establish: [AESTHETIC APPEAL] + [HOOK]. Audio:
[TRENDING AUDIO] or [ORIGINAL SOUND THAT MATCHES BRAND]. The hook should appeal to
aspirational or satisfying taste. Format for Reels algorithm (15–90 seconds ideal).
Include caption suggestion that adds context without explaining the hook. Optimize for
feed consistency if this is a branded creator.

**Instagram Reels hook-specific elements:**
- Opening should be visually gorgeous (not cheap or jarring)
- Lighting should be professional (not harsh smartphone lighting)
- Color palette should be intentional (not random clips)
- Hook should feel "safe" while being engaging (avoid shock for shock's sake)
- Include caption suggestion that drives saves/shares, not just views
- Audio: trending Reels audio gets discovery, but original audio builds brand

### YouTube Shorts Hook Optimization

YouTube Shorts algorithm values click-through (from Shorts shelf to full video or
channel), watch time, and engagement rate. Shorts viewers often come from YouTube's
ecosystem, not pure social browsing.

**YouTube Shorts-specific requirements:**
- Thumbnail frame matters (first frame should be scroll-stopping)
- Hook should create desire to click through or go to channel
- Searchable keywords matter (Shorts can be found via search)
- Hook should complement channel's existing content strategy
- Vertical format (9:16)

**Prompt template for YouTube Shorts:**
Create a YouTube Shorts hook designed to drive click-through to full video or channel
visit. First frame (0.0 s) should be "thumbnail-worthy" (will generate a preview image
in Shorts feed). Hook structure: [PATTERN INTERRUPT] → [CURIOSITY] → [PROMISE OF
SOLUTION/ANSWER]. Audio: [ENGAGING AUDIO] or [VOICE-OVER HOOK]. At 2 seconds, hook
should make viewer think "I need to click this to see the full story." Include suggested
title that includes searchable keywords. Output should include: hook script, thumbnail
frame description, suggested title with keywords, full video premise.

**YouTube Shorts hook-specific elements:**
- First frame is critical (becomes thumbnail preview)
- Hook should create unresolved tension (must click to resolve)
- Searchable keyword in title is important
- Shorts are often a doorway to longer content (hook should funnel viewers)
- Audio can include voice-over (YouTubers trust voice more than TikTokers)

## Content Category Playbook

### Comedy Hook Playbook

Comedy hooks rely on timing and surprise. The laugh must come within 2 seconds.

Prompt template:
Create a comedy hook where the setup is 0.5–1.0 seconds, and the punchline/absurd
reveal lands at 1.5–2.0 seconds. Methods: (1) exaggerated reaction to mundane thing,
(2) absurd visual (person doing something ridiculous), (3) unexpected dialog/text
punchline, (4) slapstick/physical comedy surprise, (5) misdirection (setup false
expectation, deliver absurdity). The punchline must be "fast" and "obvious" (comedy
requires clarity). Use comedic sound effects or laugh track. Character/subject should
show clear comedic intent (not accidental or mean-spirited). Output: video script with
exact timing for setup and punchline.

Comedy hook subcategories:
- **Exaggeration:** person takes mundane thing to extreme (3-minute morning routine
  compressed into 15 seconds of absurd prep)
- **Absurdity:** ridiculous premise stated matter-of-factly (why I bring a toaster to
  the beach)
- **Physical Comedy:** slapstick, clumsy moment, unexpected fall (timed to land at 1.8 s)
- **Dark Humor:** edgy joke setup, subverted expectation
- **Self-Deprecation:** person makes fun of themselves (relatable failure)

### Educational Hook Playbook

Educational hooks require curiosity more than comedy. The hook promises learning
something useful or surprising.

Prompt template:
Create an educational hook that promises to teach or reveal something the viewer did not
know. Hook structure: 0–0.5 s: establish the "unknown" (what will be taught). 0.5–1.5 s:
tease the answer or reveal first part. 1.5–2.0 s: punchline/surprising fact lands. Text
overlay at 0.7 s: "[HOOK TEXT] You probably didn't know [FACT]" or "[HOW] to [ACHIEVE]"
or "[REASON] it works." Audio: informative tone or surprising "aha" sound effect. The
hook should make viewer think "I want to keep watching to learn the rest." Output: hook
script + 1-line explanation of what will be taught in full video.

Educational hook subcategories:
- **Life Hack:** how to do something faster/easier/better
- **Science Fact:** surprising scientific truth (why you are probably doing X wrong)
- **Skills Tutorial:** first step or surprising approach to learning something
- **Myth Bust:** common belief is actually false/wrong
- **Shortcut:** faster/smarter way to achieve familiar goal

### Satisfying/ASMR Hook Playbook

Satisfying hooks trigger the ASMR response (tingles, calm focus, pleasure). First 2
seconds should activate the sensory satisfaction.

Prompt template:
Create a satisfying/ASMR hook that triggers sensory pleasure in the first 2 seconds.
Visual: something being sliced, molded, created, transformed, organized, cleaned, or
restored. Sound: ASMR audio (cutting sounds, whispers, tapping, scratching, water
sounds, crisp transitions). At 0.0–0.3 s: macro shot of texture or motion. At 0.3–1.0 s:
continue satisfying action with ASMR sound building. At 1.0–2.0 s: reveal outcome or
escalate satisfaction. The viewer should feel the satisfying tingle and not want to scroll
away. Output: script with exact ASMR sound description and video pacing.

Satisfying/ASMR hook subcategories:
- **Cleaning/Restoration:** before/after, aggressive cleaning, transformation through
  scrubbing
- **Cutting/Slicing:** satisfying cuts through material (clay, soap, sandwiches)
- **Organization:** chaos becoming order, decluttering, reorganization
- **Creation:** something being made or built from scratch
- **Nature:** natural satisfying moments (waves, waterfalls, plant growth)

### Transformation Hook Playbook

Transformation hooks show radical change: person, space, object, or situation
transformed.

Prompt template:
Create a transformation hook showing dramatic before/after or in-progress change. Hook
structure: 0.0–0.5 s: "before" state (messy, broken, unfinished, sad, unhealthy).
0.5–1.2 s: transformation beginning (action being taken, magic happening, process
starting). 1.2–2.0 s: partial reveal of "after" (enough to see change is happening,
but not full reveal). At 1.8 s, on-screen text: "WAIT FOR IT" or "THE RESULTS" or
"UNBELIEVABLE." Audio: music building, satisfying transformation sounds, or surprised
gasp. The hook should make viewer want to see the full transformation. Output: script
with exact timing for before/during/after reveal.

Transformation hook subcategories:
- **Personal Transformation:** person getting fit, gaining confidence, learning skill,
  improving appearance
- **Space Transformation:** room redecorated, home renovated, space cleared/organized
- **Object Transformation:** broken thing fixed, ugly thing beautified, trash into
  treasure
- **Skill Mastery:** beginner vs. expert doing same task
- **Health/Fitness:** before/after body or fitness achievement

### Behind-the-Scenes Hook Playbook

BTS hooks offer insider access or reveal process/truth behind something.

Prompt template:
Create a behind-the-scenes hook that reveals how something is made, prepared, or faked.
Hook structure: 0.0–0.8 s: show the "public" version or finished product. 0.8–1.5 s:
cut to "behind the scenes" revealing the machinery, prep, or truth. 1.5–2.0 s: reveal
is shocking/surprising (not what viewer expected). Text at 1.0 s: "WAIT, HERE'S THE
TRUTH" or "HOW IT'S ACTUALLY MADE" or "THIS ISN'T WHAT YOU THINK." Audio: surprising
reveal sound or comedic sound effect. The hook should make viewer think "I didn't know
that" or "that's not how I thought it worked." Output: script with exact timing for
public → backstage cut.

Behind-the-scenes hook subcategories:
- **Production Process:** how product/video/meal is made (not shown publicly)
- **Truth Reveal:** reality vs. appearance (fake vs. real, filter vs. no-filter)
- **Mistake Exposure:** what goes wrong behind the scenes, fails, retakes
- **Preparation:** effort/time required to make something look easy
- **Creator Life:** what creators do when not performing

### Reaction Hook Playbook

Reaction hooks rely on genuine or exaggerated emotional response to trigger engagement.

Prompt template:
Create a reaction hook where the reactor's face/emotion is the main event. Hook
structure: 0.0–0.8 s: setup (person reads text, watches video, hears news, receives
surprise). 0.8–1.5 s: reaction begins (eyes widen, mouth drops, tears form, shock
registers). 1.5–2.0 s: reaction intensifies or punchline moment. Framing: reaction
should dominate screen (close-up or split screen with stimulus on one side, reaction
on other). Audio: reaction sounds (gasp, laugh, scream) + background audio from
stimulus. The reaction must feel authentic or clearly comedic (not fake-seeming).
Output: script with reaction trigger and timing.

Reaction hook subcategories:
- **Text/News Reaction:** person reads shocking/funny/surprising text or message
- **Video Reaction:** person watches clip (viral moment, movie trailer, music video)
- **Food/Taste Reaction:** person tastes something unexpected, shocking, or delicious
- **Experience Reaction:** person reacts to new experience (first time VR, first ride
  on coaster)
- **Reveal Reaction:** person learns secret, sees surprise, gets unexpected news

### Storytime Hook Playbook

Storytime hooks set up a narrative that begs to be continued.

Prompt template:
Create a storytime hook that establishes a narrative with conflict, interest, or
curiosity. Hook structure: 0.0–1.0 s: establish setting and character(s). At 0.7 s:
hint at the conflict or problem ("So there I was...", "I didn't expect...", "The worst
part was..."). 1.0–1.5 s: escalate the intrigue (problem becomes clear, stakes are
raised, question emerges). 1.5–2.0 s: cliffhanger moment or question that must be
answered in full video. Text overlay: optional, can highlight key phrase ("BIGGEST
MISTAKE" or "I WASN'T PREPARED"). Audio: conversational voice-over or dialogue,
background music building emotion. The viewer should think "I need to hear the rest
of this story." Output: script with story setup, conflict, and cliffhanger.

Storytime hook subcategories:
- **Personal Mistake:** story of when person learned hard lesson
- **Unexpected Event:** surprising/shocking thing that happened
- **Plot Twist:** setup false expectation, then reveal truth
- **Overcoming Challenge:** how person faced and conquered difficulty
- **Advice/Wisdom:** story illustrating lesson to teach

### Product Hook Playbook

Product hooks showcase or reveal a product's benefit without feeling like an ad.

Prompt template:
Create a product hook that reveals product value in first 2 seconds without feeling
like a commercial. Hook structure: 0.0–0.5 s: problem is shown or implied (person
struggling, frustrated, needing something). 0.5–1.0 s: product appears or is
introduced (natural, not forced). 1.0–1.5 s: product's benefit is demonstrated
(problem solved, transformation happens, magic occurs). 1.5–2.0 s: result is revealed
(person happy, problem gone, unexpected benefit shown). Text at 1.2 s: product name
or key benefit. Audio: satisfaction sound, positive emotion, or impressed tone. The
hook should feel like a "wow" moment, not a sales pitch. Output: script with problem,
product intro, and benefit reveal timing.

Product hook subcategories:
- **Problem Solved:** person struggles, product solves problem, magic happens
- **Hidden Feature:** product does unexpected thing (secret hack or capability)
- **Comparison:** product vs. alternative (product wins in satisfying way)
- **Unboxing Reveal:** product's packaging or design is gorgeous/surprising
- **Result Showcase:** before product vs. after product (transformation)

### Lifestyle Hook Playbook

Lifestyle hooks show aspirational or relatable living, daily routines, or aesthetic
moments.

Prompt template:
Create a lifestyle hook that feels aspirational or deeply relatable. Hook structure:
0.0–0.8 s: aesthetic moment (beautiful lighting, calming activity, satisfying routine).
At 0.5 s: optional text hint at lifestyle theme ("morning routine", "slow living",
"luxury", "cozy"). 0.8–1.5 s: moment develops (routine continues, aesthetic deepens).
1.5–2.0 s: reveal or reframe (unexpected element, twist on expectation, or aspirational
escalation). Audio: ambient music, ASMR, or calming sound design that matches mood.
Cinematography: high-quality framing, intentional color palette, smooth movement. The
viewer should feel "I want this lifestyle" or "I relate to this." Output: script with
mood/aesthetic description and lifestyle theme.

Lifestyle hook subcategories:
- **Morning/Evening Routine:** calming or energizing daily ritual
- **Aesthetic Moment:** beautiful composition, satisfying visual, calming environment
- **Luxury/Aspiration:** showing high-end lifestyle, expensive items, dream living
- **Relatable Moment:** funny/true moment from everyday life that viewers recognize
- **Wellness/Health:** meditation, exercise, self-care, mental health moment

## Trending Format Templates

Master these templates. Mix them with hook patterns for maximum virality.

**Duet/Stitch Bait**
Create a hook that begs someone to respond with their own version. Format: ask question,
show your answer, make completion satisfying. Hook: 0–2 s shows the setup/question,
forces response.

**POV (Point of View) Format**
Viewer experiences the scenario as if they are the subject. Format: "POV: You just did
[embarrassing/amazing/shocking thing]." Hook: 0–2 s shows the consequence or reaction
to viewer's action. Audio: often uses second-person voice.

**Put a Finger Down**
Interactive format: "put a finger down if you've ever..." Format: list increasingly
specific or embarrassing statements, viewer judges self. Hook: 0–2 s shows first 2–3
"finger down" statements. Engagement: incredibly high (viewers mentally participate).

**GRWM (Get Ready With Me)**
Getting ready while sharing information or entertainment. Format: person gets ready
while delivering content. Hook: 0–2 s shows person at start of routine, hints at what
will be shared. Retention: naturally high (viewers want to see the finished look).

**Day in the Life**
Following routine from start to finish, showing lifestyle or job. Format: morning-to-night
or start-to-finish of specific task. Hook: 0–2 s shows most dramatic moment or biggest
reveal from the day. Often uses: time-stamps, montage pace.

**ASMR Mukbang**
Eating/drinking while producing satisfying sounds. Format: close-up of food, macro
audio, minimal talking. Hook: 0–2 s shows most delicious-looking bite or crunchiest
sound. Audio is primary (ASMR satisfying eating sounds).

**Trend Hijack**
Take trending audio/dance/format and put your own spin. Format: use trending audio but
with unexpected content. Hook: 0–2 s establishes the trend, then subverts expectation.

**Tutorial/How-To**
Teaching someone to do something. Format: problem setup, step-by-step solution, reveal
final result. Hook: 0–2 s shows the problem and hints that solution is simple/surprising.
Pacing: quick cuts through steps (montage-style).

**Transition Challenge**
Person transforms from one state to another using trending audio/music. Format: cut on
beat of music showing before/after or different versions of same person. Hook: 0–2 s
shows first state and hints transformation is coming. Audio must have clear beat to
cut on.

## Analytics-Driven Optimization

### Key Metrics to Track

**Watch Time %:**
- 100% watch time = flawless hook + retention
- 90%+ = strong hook, most viewers stay
- 80–90% = good hook, some drop-off mid-video
- Below 80% = hook is not working or video body is weak

**Watch time dips reveal retention problems:**
- If 50% drop off at 3 seconds: hook is not delivering on its promise
- If 70% drop off at 10 seconds: hook works but video body is weak
- If completion rate increases after 15 seconds: audience is invested (narrative is
  working)

**First 3 Seconds Metric:**
- Average click-through rate (CTR) = (clicks / impressions) x 100
- Higher CTR = better hook (platform shows it to more people)
- TikTok average CTR for new creators: 3–5%
- Viral threshold: 8%+ (algorithm boost kicks in)

### Iteration Based on Data

**If 0–2 s has below 70% retention:**
- Hook is weak. Try stronger pattern interrupt.
- Audio is not compelling. Try trending audio or ASMR.
- Opening frame is unclear. Try extreme close-up or bold movement.
- Text is not clear. Increase text size/contrast.

**If 2–5 s has high drop-off (retention falls from 80% to 40%):**
- Hook promised something not delivered.
- Pacing after hook is too slow.
- Context/explanation takes too long.
- Solution: deliver on hook promise faster, cut to payoff at 3 s.

**If 5–15 s has declining retention:**
- Hook works, but video body is weak.
- Curiosity is not sustained past initial hook.
- Add secondary hook at 5 s (mini-cliff before resolution).
- Increase visual energy or emotional escalation.

### A/B Testing Hooks

Create 2–3 versions of same content with different hooks:
- Version A: Curiosity gap hook (half-reveal)
- Version B: Emotional trigger hook (fear, awe, cute)
- Version C: Visual shock hook (impossible physics, color explosion)

Post each to separate accounts or same account at different times. Compare: first
3-second CTR, completion rate %, average watch time. Whichever wins identifies which
hook mechanism works best for your audience/content category.

### Platform-Specific Metrics

**TikTok:**
- Track: completion rate %, average watch time, like-share rate
- Viral signal: 80%+ completion rate + 8%+ engagement rate
- Algorithm boost kicks in at 80k+ impressions with high completion

**Instagram Reels:**
- Track: saves, shares, comments (more valuable than views)
- Viral signal: save-to-view ratio above 5% or share-to-view ratio above 2%
- Saves indicate "want to come back to this"

**YouTube Shorts:**
- Track: average view duration, click-through rate to full video
- Viral signal: 60%+ of viewers click to full video or subscribe
- Retention curves show drop-off patterns clearly
