## Hero Angle Selection by Category

Every product has a "hero angle" — the view that best communicates value, function, and
aesthetics. Identify this first before building the rotation sequence.

| Category | Primary hero angle | Secondary angle |
|----------|--------------------|-----------------|
| Watches / Rings | Front face, 35° tilt to show depth | Side profile (case thickness) |
| Bags | Three-quarter front (silhouette) | Side (structure) |
| Electronics | Screen-facing front | Rear (materials + branding) |
| Shoes | Three-quarter emphasizing profile | Sole detail |
| Jewelry | Direct light into stone facets | Rotation (setting complexity) |
| Cosmetics | Top-down (compact/bottle) | Side (proportions + design) |
| Automotive parts | 45° above (engineering geometry) | Profile (form + shadow) |
| Furniture / Home | 45° above, diffused (material + scale) | In-context lifestyle |
| Food packaging | Slight tilt (label + color) | Top-down (footprint) |

## Rotation Speed and Cadence

| Speed | Full 360° time | Best for |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| Slow | 10–15 s | Jewelry, watches, luxury goods — viewer absorbs every detail |
| Medium | 5–8 s | Fashion, cosmetics, electronics — shows form and function |
| Fast | 2–4 s | Tech gadgets, dynamic brands — communicates speed and modernity |
| Multi-speed | Varies | Start slow on hero angle, accelerate mid, slow for finale |

## Full and Partial Rotation Patterns

### Full 360° Rotations

1. **Slow Full Rotation** — 15+ s for 360°. Viewers absorb every angle. Use for jewelry,
   watches, luxury bags, complex products.
2. **Medium Full Rotation** — 6–8 s for 360°. Balanced discovery. Use for electronics,
   fashion, shoes.
3. **Fast Full Rotation** — 2–4 s for 360°. Dynamic and modern. Use for tech gadgets.
4. **Spin + Float** — 360° rotation while product slowly ascends. Use for luxury reveals.
5. **Multi-Speed Rotation** — Slow start → accelerate → slow finale. Builds narrative.
6. **Overhead Spin** — Top-down camera, product spins 360°. Use for watches, rings,
   flat products.

### Partial Rotations

7. **Half Rotation (180°)** — Front to back, reveals hidden side. Efficient surprise.
8. **Quarter Rotation (90°)** — Hero angle to side. Quick for bags, profile-focused items.
9. **Pendulum Swing** — 45° left to 45° right. Highlights front and sides without full loop.

### Camera Orbit (camera moves, product stationary)

10. **Continuous Orbital** — Camera orbits at 45° around stationary product (360°). Use
    for furniture, large products.
11. **Parallax Orbit** — Camera orbits while rotating slightly. 3D depth. Use for luxury,
    watches, jewelry.

## Dynamic Camera Movements

12. **Push-In Macro** — Camera pushes close to reveal texture, stitching, engravings.
13. **Pull-Back Reveal** — Start macro, pull back to show full product ("ah-ha!" moment).
14. **Dolly Slide (Horizontal)** — Camera slides left-to-right, shows product profile.
15. **Tilt Up / Tilt Down** — Adds dimension while product rotates. Use for tall products.
16. **Dutch Tilt Rotation** — Product rotates with camera tilted 25–35°. Use for tech,
    dynamic brands.
17. **Spin-to-Stop-Pan** — Product spins 360°, stops at hero angle, camera pans closer.
18. **Floating Rotation** — Product rotates while slowly floating up. Use for jewelry,
    cosmetics.
19. **Boom-Up Rotation** — Product rotates while camera rises vertically. Shows bottom detail.
20. **Zoom Pan Transition** — Zoom into detail, hold, then pan to show detail in full context.

## Multi-Image Angle Strategy (3–9 photos)

When the user provides multiple product images, build a comprehensive showcase:

### 3-Image
1. Front (Hero Angle) → 2. Side/Profile → 3. Detail/Macro or Back

### 5-Image
1. Front → 2. Right Side → 3. Back → 4. Left Side → 5. Detail/Macro

### 7-Image
1. Front → 2. 45° Right → 3. Right Side → 4. Back →
5. 45° Left → 6. Left Side → 7. Detail + Lifestyle

### 9-Image
1. Front → 2. Front-Right 30° → 3. Right Side → 4. Back-Right 30° → 5. Back →
6. Back-Left 30° → 7. Left Side → 8. Front-Left 30° → 9. Detail/Material + Lifestyle

**Timing guidance:** 1–2 s per image standard; 0.5–1 s rapid-fire; 2–3 s for detailed
angles. Use dissolves or camera moves to connect images. Maintain lighting continuity.

## Per-Category Lighting Guide

### Electronics and Tech Hardware

- **Key Light:** 45° above, slightly forward, cool-toned (5600K). Sharp specular highlights
  on screens and metal bezels.
- **Fill Light:** 50% intensity, opposite side, slightly warm (4000K). Adds dimension.
- **Rim Light:** Subtle edge light separating product from background.
- **Background:** Gradient dark gray to white. Neutral, corporate.
- **Special:** Light reflections in screens and glass panels communicate capability.

### Jewelry and Watches

- **Key Light:** 30° to side, slightly above. Single, directional, hard-edged.
  Creates brilliant specular highlights on facets and metal.
- **Fill Light:** Minimal. Shadows enhance drama and dimension.
- **Rim Light:** Strong. Halo effect around edges, emphasizing detail.
- **Background:** Black, deep navy, or mirror. Reflects light, creates backdrop sparkle.
- **Special:** Rotate product through light so facets "flash" every half-second of rotation.

### Fashion Accessories (Bags, Belts, Scarves)

- **Key Light:** 45° above and to side, warm (3200–4000K). Creates soft depth.
- **Fill Light:** 60% intensity, same warmth. Maintains texture without harsh shadows.
- **Rim Light:** Moderate. Separates material from background.
- **Special:** Let light graze across surfaces to show stitching, grain, and patina.

### Cosmetics and Perfume

- **Key Light:** 60° above, diffused, warm (3200K). Soft, shadowless illumination.
- **Fill Light:** 80% intensity. Maintains overall softness.
- **Rim Light:** Subtle. Just enough to separate from background.
- **Special:** Emphasize transparency, color gradations, and shine on glass.

### Watches (Dedicated)

- **Key Light:** 35° above, precise angle. Cool-toned for metal and crystal.
- **Fill Light:** 50% intensity. Maintains dial readability and hand detail.
- **Rim Light:** Moderate-to-strong. Separation between watch and background.
- **Background:** Black or deep navy for high contrast.
- **Special:** Rotate to show dial → case profile → caseback. Light catches on crystal.

### Footwear

- **Key Light:** 45° above and to side, warm (3500K). Emphasizes shape.
- **Fill Light:** 60% intensity. Texture detail in creases and folds.
- **Rim Light:** Moderate. Profile separation.
- **Special:** Showcase sole, tread, materials. Side profile first, then top, then detail.

### Furniture and Home Goods

- **Key Light:** 45° above, diffused, warm (3200–3500K). Emphasizes wood, fabric, leather.
- **Fill Light:** 70% intensity. Overall balance.
- **Rim Light:** Subtle. Soft separation.
- **Special:** Show size with hand interaction or in-use context.

### Food Packaging and Consumables

- **Key Light:** 45° above, warm (3200K). Appetite appeal and warmth.
- **Fill Light:** 70% intensity. Prevents harsh shadows.
- **Special:** Emphasize color saturation and vibrancy. If window, show product inside.

### Automotive Parts and Accessories

- **Key Light:** 45° above, dramatic. Strong reflection and shadow contrast.
- **Fill Light:** 40% intensity. Detail in shadow areas.
- **Rim Light:** Strong. Separation and emphasis on form.
- **Special:** Multiple light sources for metallic reflections. Communicate speed and precision.

## Material and Surface Showcase Techniques

| Material | Lighting approach | Key technique |
|----------|------------------|---------------|
| Metal (stainless, gold, aluminum) | Hard, directional key | Let specular highlights "dance" across surface during rotation |
| Glass and Crystal | Multi-directional | Emphasize clarity and refraction; show caustic effects if liquid-filled |
| Leather and Hide | Warm, angled to graze surface | Emphasize patina and grain; slow rotation with pauses |
| Fabric (weave, knit, silk) | Soft, 45° to show weave | Medium rotation; let fabric catch and release light |
| Wood (grain, finish) | Warm, diffused | Slow rotation; let viewers appreciate grain nuance |
| Ceramic and Porcelain | Soft, directional | Medium rotation; light catches on glossy areas |
| Liquid (perfume, beverage) | Cool, from behind/side | Emphasize color saturation and refraction |
| Matte surfaces | Soft, diffuse | Medium rotation; flatness communicates sophistication |
| Glossy surfaces | Hard, directional | Medium-to-slow; specular highlights move across surface |
