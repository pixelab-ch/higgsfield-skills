## Appetite Appeal Philosophy

Every frame serves one purpose: **make the viewer's mouth water and stomach rumble**.

- **Texture is taste on screen.** Crispy exteriors vs creamy interiors; smooth sauces
  clinging to protein; rough salt crystals on caramel; the flake of a pastry crust.
- **Steam and heat signal freshness.** Rising vapour from soup, mist on a bowl,
  condensation on glassware, the moment a lid lifts.
- **Colour vibrancy without artifice.** Golden-brown proteins (Maillard reaction);
  deep greens and reds of fresh vegetables; jewel tones of berries, peppers, beets.
- **Close-up intimacy sells.** Macro shots of texture, POV from the eater's perspective,
  extreme close-ups of sauce or glaze, depth of field that isolates the hero.
- **The money shot is the climactic moment** that makes viewers say "I want that."
  Design the entire video to build toward this moment.
- **Sound is taste proxy.** Sizzle = heat; crunch = freshness; pour = indulgence;
  fizz = refreshment; chop = skill and care.

## Lighting Playbook

### Warm Key Light (45° angle)
Golden-hour warmth (2700 K–3200 K). Fills in details without harsh shadows; makes
food inviting. Side-angle at 45° is flattering for texture.

### Backlight for Steam, Glaze, and Gloss
Position light behind the subject opposite the key light. Backlit steam glows golden;
backlit glaze or oil becomes luminous; backlit bubbles shine. Creates separation and
depth. Essential for dramatic food presentation.

### Side Light for Texture and Dimension
Light grazing across the food surface. Reveals crumb structure, crust, sear marks.
Creates shadow-and-highlight play; adds 3D feel. Makes rough-vs-smooth contrast visible.

### Natural Window Light (Soft and Diffused)
Soft directional light; no harsh shadows. Ideal for fresh, bright aesthetics — salads,
smoothies, pastries. Cool-to-neutral colour temperature. Intimate, approachable.

### Candlelight Ambiance (Dinner Scenes)
Warm, intimate tungsten glow. Flatters skin and food. Creates luxury / celebration mood.
Works for fine dining and upscale beverage scenes.

## Movement and Action Vocabulary

### Hands Preparing
- Knife work (chopping, slicing, dicing)
- Ingredient washing, peeling, or husking
- Tearing herbs or bread
- Mixing, kneading, whisking, beating

### Hands Plating
- Placing protein on plate with precision
- Spooning sauce with a deliberate arc
- Tweezers placing microgreens or edible flowers
- Hands framing the finished dish

### Pouring and Drizzling
- Sauce from spoon or container (slow-motion for impact)
- Oil or dressing in a thin stream
- Beverage into glass (slow-motion is key)
- Soup or broth into bowl; glaze over pastry

### Cutting and Slicing
- Knife through protein revealing interior colour
- Cake or bread slicing showing layers
- Fruit cutting showing juice explosion
- Cross-section reveals; the moment blade contacts food

### Stirring and Mixing
- Wooden spoon through sauce
- Whisk through cream or eggs
- Tossing ingredients in pan
- Cocktail shaker technique in slow-motion

### Serving and Presentation
- Plate being set before camera
- Lid being lifted to reveal
- Garnish placed at last moment
- The "reveal" moment

### Eating and Tasting
- Fork into mouth; sip from cup or glass
- Bite with jaw motion (not closed-mouthed)
- Savoring expression; napkin moment

## Money Shot Library

These are the climactic moments that make viewers stop scrolling. Use these exact
framings in Higgsfield prompts:

**1. Cheese Pull at Maximum Stretch** — Melted mozzarella being pulled from a slice,
strand stretching 12+ inches with backlit glow, captured in smooth slow-motion from
the side angle, showing the cheese reaching maximum elasticity before breaking.

**2. Sauce Pour with Glossy Cascade** — Rich sauce poured from a spoon onto a protein
or dessert, sauce cascading and pooling on white plate, golden backlight making liquid
glow, steam rising from the hot sauce.

**3. Cake Slice Reveal with Interior Crumb Visible** — Knife cutting cleanly through
multi-layer cake in one smooth motion, revealing distinct interior layers (sponge,
frosting, filling), crumb detail visible, slow push-in camera as slice separates.

**4. Drink Pour with Ice Interaction** — Spirit or beverage poured over ice cubes in
crystal glass, liquid colour prominent, ice cubes glinting and shifting, slow-motion
pour showing liquid path and spread, condensation forming on glass exterior.

**5. Steam Cloud Backlit and Glowing** — Hot food steaming, vapour rising and backlit
by warm golden light, steam illuminated like glowing gold, macro detail of steam wisps,
creating ethereal glow around the dish.

**6. Ingredient Cascade / Rain** — Fresh herbs or garnish rained down from above,
each element falling and landing, captured in slow-motion, dust and micro-particles
visible in the light.

**7. Oil or Drizzle Pooling** — Premium olive oil, balsamic, or truffle oil drizzled
onto food in a slow deliberate line, liquid spreading and pooling, sheen and shine
catching light, creating an oil slick effect that signals luxury.

**8. Chocolate Fountain or Flow** — Melted chocolate flowing or pouring from height
onto a surface, smooth chocolate stream captured in macro detail, chocolate pooling
and coating the target, glossy chocolate surface.

**9. Bread or Pastry Tear with Steam Release** — Hands tearing into fresh bread or
croissant, interior exposed, steam releasing from the warm crumb, tear texture visible
(shaggy or laminated layers), crumb detail in sharp focus.

**10. Fruit Splash or Juice Explosion** — Fresh fruit being cut or bitten, juice
splashing or spurting, droplets caught mid-air, vibrant fruit colours glistening.

**11. Crispy Exterior Crunch Moment** — Knife or fork cutting into crispy-exterior /
creamy-interior food, crispy exterior shattering, interior oozing, sound of crunch
layered in, macro of the cut surface.

**12. Sizzle on Hot Pan with Colour Change** — Protein or vegetable hitting
screaming-hot pan, immediate sizzle and steam, colour change from raw to seared
visible, oil jumping, close-up of sear surface and browning.

**13. Fork or Spoon Through Yielding Protein** — Fork cutting effortlessly through
perfectly cooked protein (butter-tender steak, flaky fish), fork separating piece,
interior revealed, possibly with juice or glaze, tenderness immediately apparent.

**14. Glaze or Icing Drip** — Viscous glaze or icing poured over pastry, donut, or
cake, liquid dripping down sides in slow-motion, shine catching light, drips pooling
at base.

**15. First Bite or Sip Reaction** — Hands bringing food or drink to mouth, the moment
before contact, bite taken or sip sipped, satisfaction visible in the action, sensory
pleasure evident.

## Food Styling Tricks

- **Gloss and sheen:** Brush protein with oil or glaze to catch light (strategic, not
  greasy).
- **Seasoning and texture:** Sea salt crystals, cracked pepper, fresh herbs visible
  (not overdone).
- **Condensation:** Water spray on glassware or vegetables for freshness signal.
- **Negative space:** Minimalist plating with intentional empty plate space (fine
  dining).
- **Layering:** Stack ingredients so each layer is visible (height and complexity).
- **Colour contrast:** Place reds next to greens, yellows next to purples (natural
  colour harmony).
- **Charring marks:** Grill or sear marks signal technique and heat.
- **Steam timing:** Plate hot foods right before the shot to capture peak steam.
- **Garnish freshness:** Use herbs moments before shooting — wilting is visible.
- **Props:** Wood cutting boards, ceramic, marble, linen — elevated surfaces elevate
  food.

## Colour Palettes by Cuisine

| Cuisine / style | Dominant palette |
|---|---|
| Fine dining | Whites, blacks, golds, jewel tones, deep greens |
| Fast food / casual | Saturated reds, warm oranges, golden yellows, bright greens |

| Baked goods / pastry | Golden browns, creams, whites, butter yellows |

## Sound Design and ASMR Layering

Layer sounds strategically — one dominant sound per action with subtle background
ambience underneath.

| Sound | Signal | Pairing |
|---|---|---|
| Sizzle | Heat, protein, caramelisation | Searing, stir-fry, BBQ |
| Crunch | Freshness, texture, quality | Biting, chopping, breaking |
| Pour | Indulgence, flow, generosity | Sauces, beverages, syrups |
| Fizz / bubble | Refreshment, energy, celebration | Carbonated drinks, champagne |
| Chop | Skill, freshness, care | Knife work, vegetable prep |
| Bubble (simmering) | Transformation, magic | Stocks, candy, jam |
| Crackle | Caramelisation, char, crust | Brûlée torch, smoked meats |
| Slurp | Indulgence, satisfaction | Noodles, soups, beverages |

**Volume layering guidance:**
- ASMR/dominant sound: 30–50 % volume
- Background music: 50–70 % volume (lower under ASMR moments)
- Ambient background: 20–40 % volume
- Voiceover (if used): 80–100 % volume

## Content Strategy by Request Type

### Restaurant / Location Promo
Goal: drive foot traffic; show *experience* and *ambiance*, not just food.
Include chef/barista hands (craft visible), plating action, ambient lighting,
multiple dishes, possible table reveal shot. Sound: ambiance-forward.

### Packaged Food Product
Goal: drive retail purchase; show *product quality* and *difference from competitors*.
Isolate product on clean background; close-ups of texture and quality cues; show
product open or prepared; packaging prominent. Sound: product-focused (crunch, tear,
bite, satisfaction).

### Recipe / How-To Content
Goal: educational + entertaining; teach while making viewers hungry.
Show technique clearly; multiple angles of each action; ingredient close-ups as
introduced; timing cues visible; final plated result. Sound: educational; layer every
cooking sound.

## Common Mistakes and Fixes

**Food looks sad or unappetizing:** Add backlight to make sauces and gloss shine;
use warm colour temp; ensure steam is visible and backlit; add gloss strategically;
use vibrant colours and high contrast.

**Cheese pull doesn't stretch:** Ensure cheese is fully melted; shoot from 45° angle;
use slow-motion; backlight the cheese strand; isolate the moment full-frame.

**Sound design is distracting:** Layer sounds — one dominant + subtle background;
match sound to action; keep ambience low and supportive; avoid cartoon-loud effects.

**Camera movement is jerky:** Use smooth, slow, predictable movements; let the food
action be the star; avoid more than one camera movement per shot; slow push-in is
safer than dynamic orbits.

**Harsh shadows on food:** Add fill light or bounce board; position food at 45° to
light; use diffusion; layer key + backlight + side light.

**Plating looks cluttered:** Rule of 3 — 3 main elements max; leave negative space;
one focal point (the money-shot element); colour harmony (warm OR cool, not both).

**Steam doesn't show:** Always backlight steam; shoot in slightly lower ambient
light; film the moment steam is strongest; use macro lens for fine wisps.

**Food looks overprocessed:** Keep colour grading warm and natural; use real textures;
avoid over-saturation; include natural imperfections (char variation, real crumb).

**Hook is too slow:** Start mid-action; cheese pull — begin as bun lifts (not before);
sauce pour — start as liquid is positioned and ready; make the first frame intriguing.

**Pacing mismatch:** Fine dining = slower; fast food = energetic; casual = medium.
Use slow-motion for money shots only; regular speed for context. Check total length
feels complete — not rushed, not dragging.
