## Choreography Philosophy

### Specific Movement vs. Vague Action

**Weak:** "They fight intensely."

**Strong:** "The defender flows backward in three precise steps — each footfall perfectly
timed — while raising their forearms to parry descending overhead strikes from the
aggressor. On the third parry, they pivot 45 degrees and deliver a sharp elbow to the
aggressor's ribs."

Specificity creates visual clarity and allows the model to render authentic momentum.

### Camera as Active Participant

The camera is not a bystander; it's a combatant in the scene.

- During aggressive moves, push the camera TOWARD the action (simulate aggressor's
  momentum).
- During defensive moments, pull back or pan laterally to show spatial relationships.
- Use orbital motion around static combatants during deadlock/grapple moments.
- Whip-pans between multiple fighters create chaos and confusion (mirroring the
  fighter's perspective).

### Impact and Atmosphere Sell the Punch

Don't just describe the technique. Describe what happens:

- **Dust clouds:** Kicked up from footwork, impacts, and blocking
- **Fabric ripple:** Clothing flutters and snaps from wind of strikes
- **Hair and sweat:** Flies from centrifugal force of spinning strikes
- **Skin response:** Muscle definition visible in extreme poses; beads of sweat glint in light
- **Environmental damage:** Cracked earth, splintered wood, shattered stone from missed strikes

### Tension and Release Rhythm

All great fights oscillate: buildup → impact → brief calm → buildup. Structure prompts
this way:

1. **Tension Building:** Circling, footwork, feints, breathing, narrowing distance
2. **Release/Impact:** The strike, block, or collision — 2–3 seconds of maximum action
3. **Recovery:** Brief moment of regrouping, reassessment, repositioning
4. **Acceleration:** Repeat but escalate intensity

## Fight Style Encyclopedia

### Unarmed Combat

**Kung Fu (Wushu)**
- Signature: Spinning kicks, open-palm strikes, low sweeps, flowing evasion
- Footwork: Quick, shuffling, multidirectional
- Renders: Graceful transitions, tornado rotations, ground-to-standing flips
- Prompt phrase: "flowing kung fu exchanges with spinning crescent kicks and rapid palm strikes"

**Karate**
- Signature: Straight punches, high kicks, knife-hand strikes, low stances
- Footwork: Linear, sharp, explosive from stationary positions
- Renders: Precise stances, devastating focus, snapping movements
- Prompt phrase: "sharp karate strikes with deep rooted stances and snapping full-body rotation"

**Muay Thai (Kickboxing)**
- Signature: Elbows, knees, shin kicks, clinch work, body shots
- Footwork: Bouncing, lateral movement, close-range engagement
- Renders: Brutal clinch exchanges, spinning elbows, devastating shin impacts
- Prompt phrase: "brutal muay thai clinch work with flying elbows and devastating knee strikes"

**Capoeira**
- Signature: Inverted kicks, handstands, flowing evasion, low sweeps
- Footwork: Rhythmic, ground-level, constant circular motion
- Renders: Acrobatic reversals, inverted momentum, sand clouds
- Prompt phrase: "acrobatic capoeira with inverted kicks and flowing ground-level sweeps"

**Mixed Martial Arts (MMA)**
- Signature: Striking + grappling synthesis, clinch takedowns, ground-and-pound
- Footwork: Aggressive closing distance, wrestling base
- Renders: Rapid transitions between standing/ground, scrambling, submissions
- Prompt phrase: "explosive MMA exchanges mixing striking combos with clinch wrestling"

**Taekwondo**
- Signature: High flying kicks, spinning heel kicks, fast hand combinations
- Footwork: Bouncy, explosive vertical extension
- Renders: Soaring kicks, mid-air rotations, impact landing
- Prompt phrase: "explosive taekwondo with soaring flying kicks and spinning heel strikes"

**Judo / Wrestling**
- Signature: Hip throws, arm drags, takedowns, ground scramble
- Footwork: Close, gripping, explosive hip engagement
- Renders: Dynamic throws showing arc and impact, rolling momentum
- Prompt phrase: "explosive judo throws with dramatic arc and momentum-carrying rolls"

### Weapon Combat

**Sword / Katana / Longsword**
- Signature: Overhead slashes, thrusts, parries, ripostes, lateral cuts
- Footwork: Lunging, pivoting, two-handed engagement
- Renders: Blade trails, sparks from clashes, realistic weight shifts
- Prompt phrase: "precise katana exchanges with overhead slashes and counter-thrusts, sparks flying"

**Dual Blades / Twin Swords**
- Signature: Alternating slashes, figure-eight patterns, crossing guard, simultaneous strikes
- Footwork: Tight, whirling, maximizing blade coverage
- Renders: Blade blur, intricate patterns, visual complexity
- Prompt phrase: "dual-sword combat with alternating slashes and intricate figure-eight patterns"

**Spear / Polearm**
- Signature: Thrusts, sweeps, vertical strikes, two-handed blocking
- Footwork: Controlled distance, extended reach
- Renders: Blade rotation, reach advantage, broad sweeping arcs
- Prompt phrase: "spear combat with controlled thrusts and broad sweeping arcs maintaining distance"

**Staff / Bo Staff**
- Signature: Overhead strikes, lateral sweeps, rapid two-handed spins, low sweeps
- Footwork: Spinning in place, extended reach, rotational power
- Renders: Rapid spins, blur of staff, impact shockwaves
- Prompt phrase: "bo staff mastery with rapid spinning strikes and overhead sweeps"

**Dual Pistols / Gun-Fu**
- Signature: Akimbo rapid-fire, mid-air reload, sliding, dynamic positioning
- Footwork: Parkour-influenced, evasive, high-speed repositioning
- Renders: Muzzle flashes, shell casings, dynamic camera movement
- Prompt phrase: "explosive gun-fu with dual-pistol akimbo fire and dynamic evasive sliding"

### Fantasy and Superhero Combat

**Elemental Magic (Fire, Ice, Lightning)**
- Signature: Projectile blasts, environmental elemental reactions, hand gesture casting
- Renders: Energy effects, environmental reactions, visual trails
- Prompt phrase: "fire elemental magic with explosive blast patterns and scorched earth effects"

**Superhero / Enhanced Physiology**
- Signature: Super-strength impacts, accelerated speed blurs, flight-based diving kicks
- Renders: Impact shockwaves, ground cracking, speed blur, weightless moments
- Prompt phrase: "superhero combat with explosive impacts that crack concrete and propel bodies backward"

## Choreography Keywords Library

### Striking Techniques

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Jab** | Quick, short-range punch — snap retraction |
| **Cross** | Rear-hand straight punch — full hip rotation |
| **Hook** | Curved punch to the side — rotational power |
| **Roundhouse** | Circular leg strike — maximum reach and power |
| **Axe Kick** | Vertical descending leg strike — downward crushing force |
| **Spinning Heel Kick** | Full-body rotation with trailing leg — deceptive reach |
| **Flying Kick** | Aerial ascending strike — mid-air contact |
| **Elbow Strike** | Close-range rotating elbow — sharp impact |
| **Knee Strike** | Upward driving knee — clinch engagement |
| **Open-Palm Strike** | Flat-hand forward force — penetrative stunning power |
| **Knife-Hand Strike** | Edge of hand — precision striking |

### Defensive Techniques

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Parry** | Blade-to-blade lateral deflection — maintaining offensive flow |
| **Block** | Forearm or limb absorption — stance-based stability |
| **Sidestep** | Lateral evasion — maintaining distance and angle |
| **Duck** | Low horizontal crouch — head-level strike avoidance |
| **Weave** | Rhythmic side-to-side head movement — multiple incoming strikes |
| **Roll** | Full-body evasive rotation — aerial strike avoidance |
| **Backstep** | Rapid retreat — extending distance |

### Grapple and Close-Range

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Clinch** | Two-handed upper body lock — controlling opponent |
| **Throw** | Hip or momentum-based takedown — dramatic arc to ground |
| **Grapple** | Locked wrestling exchange — ground dominance position |
| **Takedown** | Explosive leg-based floor transition — off-balance disruption |
| **Sweep** | Low circular leg strike — balance disruption from ground position |

### Advanced Sequences

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Combo** | 3+ linked strikes — alternating power sources |
| **Counter** | Immediate response to incoming strike — turned defense into offense |
| **Feint** | Fake movement — exploiting defensive response |
| **Riposte** | Parry followed by immediate counter-strike — flow continuation |
| **Aerial Reversal** | Mid-air body positioning change — impossible-angle escape |
| **Spinning Strike** | Full-body rotational momentum — maximum reach and power |

## Camera Techniques for Action

Match camera work to choreography intensity.

### Dynamic Movement

| Move | Description | When to Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Dolly Forward** | Push camera directly toward impact point | Simulate aggressor momentum |
| **Dolly Backward** | Pull camera away during grapple/clinch | Show spatial relationships |
| **Lateral Tracking** | Pan side-to-side following lateral movement | Maintain subject center |
| **Orbital Orbit** | Circle combatants during stationary exchanges | Reveal positioning |
| **Whip-Pan** | Rapid horizontal camera swing between combatants | Multi-fighter chaotic perspective |
| **Snap Zoom** | Rapid in/out focus shift | Emphasize impact moment |
| **Reverse Dolly** | Move camera backward while combatant advances | Deceptive speed perception |

### Static Positioning

| Position | Description | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Dutch Angle** | Tilt camera 20–30 degrees off horizontal | Dynamic tension, disorientation |
| **Low Angle (Worm's Eye)** | Camera at knee/floor level | Aggressor towering, power imbalance |
| **High Angle (Bird's Eye)** | Camera overhead | Combatants look smaller, environmental awareness |
| **Close-Up on Impact** | Extreme tight frame on strike contact | Exaggerate impact sensation |
| **Wide Master Shot** | Full fight visible | Spatial relationship clarity |

### Focus and Exposure

| Technique | Description | When to Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Rack Focus** | Shift sharp focus from background to foreground | Draw attention to foreground combatant |
| **Shallow DOF** | Soft background — isolate primary combatant | Focus on one fighter |
| **Deep Focus** | Sharp focus front to back | Show environmental stakes |
| **Motion Blur** | Intentional blur on fast-moving limbs | Emphasize speed |
| **Slow-Motion** | Key impacts at 0.5x–0.25x speed | Crystallize moment of contact |
| **Handheld Shake** | Subtle camera vibration during impacts | Physical realism |
| **POV Cam** | Camera as primary combatant's eyes | Subjective intensity |

## Impact Effects Encyclopedia

The FEELING of impact separates mediocre from masterful fight scenes. Layer these effects:

### Dust and Particulate

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Ground Dust Cloud** | Localized burst from foot strikes, body impacts, or landing |
| **Swirling Vortex Dust** | Spinning strikes create visible dust vortex |
| **Kicked-Up Debris** | Loose earth, sand, or gravel sprayed directionally |
| **Cloth Dust Release** | Strike to heavily clothed opponent releases minor dust burst |

### Light and Spark Effects

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Metal Spark Burst** | Weapon-to-weapon contact creates orange/white sparks — radial spray |
| **Impact Flash** | Bright white flash at moment of contact — crystallizes impact timing |
| **Elemental Glow** | Fire (orange), ice (blue), lightning (white) casting light on combatants |
| **Slow-Burn Fire Trail** | Elemental strikes leave momentary energy trails in air |

### Physical Reactions

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Slow-Motion Contact** | 0.25x–0.5x speed of actual strike contact — exaggerate deformation |
| **Hair/Clothing Ripple** | Fabric and hair fluttering from wind of strike (even before contact) |
| **Sweat Spray** | Beads of sweat flung from rapid head movement or spin |
| **Muscle Definition** | Extreme lighting/definition during power poses — show strain |

### Shock and Concussion

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Shockwave Ring** | Invisible expanding pressure wave visible as distortion — ground-based impacts |
| **Screen Shake** | Subtle camera vibration synced to impact — physical presence reinforcement |
| **Knockback Momentum** | Striking character propelled backward — momentum conservation |
| **Stagger Recovery** | Hit character staggers in stunned recovery |

### Environmental Destruction

| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Ground Cracks** | Spider-web fracture patterns radiating from impact point |
| **Stone/Wood Splintering** | Material fragmentation showing strike penetration force |
| **Vegetation Destruction** | Kicked-up leaves, severed branches, flattened grass |
| **Water Displacement** | Ripples, splashes, or geysers from water-surface impacts |

## Environment and Arena Guide

Location shapes combat drastically. Establish setting before combat begins.

### Natural Environments

| Setting | Combat Benefits | Prompt Enhancement |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Rain-Soaked Courtyard** | Water reflects impact light; rain disruption reveals wind; slippery footing | "on a rain-slicked stone courtyard, water splashing from footwork and impacts" |
| **Bamboo Forest** | Vertical line emphasis; bamboo splitting from strikes; mystical atmosphere | "through a dense bamboo grove, stalks splitting and exploding from impact" |
| **Sandy Beach** | Sand clouds visible with every footstep; golden hour lighting is cinematic | "on sun-drenched sandy beach, every strike kicking up sand clouds that glow against sunset" |
| **Mountain Peak / Cliff Edge** | Dramatic backdrop; environmental stakes; height danger | "on a windswept mountain peak, one wrong move sending combatants toward the cliff edge" |
| **Dense Urban Alley** | Narrow space forces close-range combat; tight angles | "in a cramped rain-slicked alley, combatants bouncing off brick walls" |

### Constructed Environments

| Setting | Combat Benefits | Prompt Enhancement |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Ancient Stone Temple** | Geometric lighting through columns; weathered stone shows impact marks | "inside an ancient temple, stone pillars cracking from missed strikes" |
| **Rooftop at Night** | Neon city lights below; dramatic height; vulnerable footing | "on a rain-slicked rooftop overlooking neon-lit city streets below" |
| **Gladiatorial Arena** | Circular spatial constraint; grandstand shadows; sand arena | "in a sprawling colosseum arena, sand kicked up in towering clouds" |
| **Burning Building** | Dynamic fire lighting; smoke obscuration; environmental danger | "inside a burning warehouse, firelight casting wild shadows on combatants" |
| **Frozen Lake / Snow Field** | Ice cracking from impact; snow spray; slippery footing | "on a frozen lake, ice spiderwebbing from shockwave impacts" |

## Multi-Character Fight Coordination

### 2v1 Structure

**Spatial Positioning:** Define one combatant as PRIMARY (camera focus). The two
attackers use flanking positions (left-front and right-back relative to camera).

**Orchestration:**
- Attacker A initiates strike
- Defender blocks/evades
- While defender is committed to A, Attacker B attacks from opposite angle
- Defender must choose: sacrifice one angle or perform advanced defensive move
- Rotate initiative frequently to show desperation

**Prompt example:**
"Two mercenaries attack the lone Swordmaster simultaneously from opposite sides.
Left mercenary lunges with spear; right mercenary circling for rear access. Swordmaster
spins 180 degrees, parries the spear, and backflips to reposition. Camera orbits to
show three-way spatial triangle."

### Wave Attacks (Sequential Engagement)

Structure as waves for narrative escalation and visual clarity:

1. **Wave 1:** Single attacker initiates
2. **Defender dispatches** (or stalemates)
3. **Wave 2:** Two fresh attackers
4. **Defender adapts strategy** (or looks exhausted)
5. **Wave 3:** Three simultaneous attackers converge
6. **Climactic coordination moment**

### Group Battles (3+ Combatants)

**Avoid ambiguity:** Specify which combatant is fighting which at each moment. "Group of
five warriors fighting" is chaos. "Three guards focus on Central Defender, while
Defender's ally flanks left guard from behind" is clear.

**Sectioning the frame:** Divide the screen into combat zones. Foreground action is
primary; background combats are secondary (slightly soft focus). This prevents
overwhelming visual noise.
